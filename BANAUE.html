Banaue Rice Terraces

Nestled amidst the picturesque Cordillera mountain range in the northern Philippines lies the captivating Banaue Rice Terraces. Carved into the mountainsides over 2,000 years ago by the indigenous Ifugao people, these terraces are a testament to ancient engineering and agricultural ingenuity. Often referred to as the "Eighth Wonder of the World," the terraces showcase the harmonious relationship between humans and nature.

Visitors are treated to breathtaking vistas of cascading rice paddies that stretch as far as the eye can see, creating a mesmerizing landscape that is both humbling and awe-inspiring. Beyond its stunning beauty, the Banaue Rice Terraces offer a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage of the Ifugao people, with traditional villages dotting the surrounding hillsides and opportunities to learn about age-old farming techniques.

Banaue offers a range of accommodations to suit every traveler's needs, from budget-friendly guesthouses to cozy homestays and luxurious mountain resorts. Visitors can immerse themselves in the local culture by staying in traditional Ifugao huts or opt for modern amenities with hotels offering stunning views of the terraces. As for activities, exploring the terraces on foot or via guided tours is a must, allowing visitors to appreciate the intricate irrigation system and learn about the Ifugao farming practices.

Additionally, cultural shows and performances provide insight into the indigenous heritage of the region, while trekking opportunities abound for those seeking adventure amidst the breathtaking landscapes of the Cordilleras. Whether it's witnessing the sunrise over the terraces or interacting with the warm and hospitable locals, a visit to the Banaue Rice Terraces promises an unforgettable experience that celebrates both nature and culture.

How to get there

By Air:

The nearest airport is in Baguio City, the Loakan Airport. From Baguio, you can take a bus or hire a private vehicle to Banaue. The journey takes approximately 5-6 hours.

By Land:

From Manila, take a bus bound for Banaue. Several bus companies offer trips to Banaue, with varying schedules and prices. The bus ride from Manila to Banaue takes around 9-10 hours, depending on traffic conditions.
