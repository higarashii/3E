BULUSAN LAKE

Bulusan Lake is a picturesque crater lake located in the municipality of Bulusan, Sorsogon province, in the Philippines.

The lake is renowned for its tranquil surroundings and scenic beauty. Surrounded by forested slopes, Bulusan Lake offers visitors a serene environment to relax and enjoy nature.

Hiking Trails: The Bulusan Volcano Natural Park offers hiking trails that lead to viewpoints overlooking the lake. These trails also provide opportunities for nature walks, birdwatching, and wildlife observation.

Ecotourism Destination: Bulusan Lake and its surrounding natural park are popular ecotourism destinations in the Philippines. The area is known for its rich biodiversity, including endemic species of plants and animals.

Conservation Efforts: Efforts are in place to conserve and protect Bulusan Lake and its surrounding ecosystem. Conservation initiatives focus on sustainable tourism practices, habitat restoration, and environmental education.

How to get there

By Air:

If you're traveling from outside the province or internationally, your journey will likely begin with a flight to Legazpi City Airport (also known as Bicol International Airport) in Albay province. From there, you can proceed to Bulusan by land.

By Land:

From Legazpi City: Upon arriving at Legazpi City Airport, you can take a bus, van, or jeepney bound for Sorsogon City. These are readily available at the airport or the nearby terminal. Travel time from Legazpi City to Sorsogon City is approximately 2-3 hours, depending on traffic and road conditions.

From Sorsogon City: Once in Sorsogon City, you can take another bus, van, or jeepney bound for Bulusan town. These modes of transportation are available at the bus terminal or designated pick-up points in the city. Travel time from Sorsogon City to Bulusan town is around 1.5-2 hours.

Local Transportation to Bulusan Lake:

Upon reaching Bulusan town, you can hire a tricycle or motorbike (habal-habal) to take you to Bulusan Lake. Tricycles are the common mode of transportation within the town and can usually be found near the town center or bus terminal.

Alternatively, you can inquire with your accommodation in Bulusan if they offer transportation services to tourist attractions such as Bulusan Lake.

Road Directions:

It's advisable to have a map or GPS navigation app to guide you from Bulusan town to Bulusan Lake. The lake is located within the Bulusan Volcano Natural Park, and there are signage and directions along the way to guide visitors.

Travel Tips:

Check the local transportation schedule in advance, especially if you're traveling by bus or van, as departure times may vary.

Plan your trip during the dry season (typically from November to April) to avoid potential travel disruptions due to heavy rains.

Consider hiring a local guide if you're unfamiliar with the area or prefer assistance in navigating the trails around Bulusan Lake.

By following these steps, you should be able to reach Bulusan Lake and enjoy its natural beauty and recreational activities.
