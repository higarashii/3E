Bangas Island

Bangas Island, located in Panglima Tahil, Sulu Province, is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. Here are some fascinating details about this captivating island.Bangas Island boasts crystalline white sands and crystal-clear waters that gently lap against the shoreline. Its pristine beaches easily rival other popular beach destinations in the Philippines.

Beneath the surface, vibrant coral reefs thrive around Bangas Island. This makes it an ideal spot for scuba diving and snorkeling. Exploring the underwater world here reveals a diverse marine ecosystem teeming with life.

Bangas Island is not just about natural beauty; it’s also home to a local community. Visitors can immerse themselves in the culture, interact with friendly locals, and learn about the island’s history and traditions.

Interestingly, Bangas Island serves as the resting place for the Sama and Badjao communities. Locals refer to it as Bukitkat, while tourists sometimes call it Graveyard Island due to its role as a burial ground. The tombs of the young are marked with wooden artifacts, while others are secured within open huts.

How to get there

To reach Bangas Island, you can follow these general directions:

By Public Transportation:

1.Fly or take a ferry to Jolo: You can fly to Jolo from Zamboanga City with Philippine Airlines, which operates flights three times a week and the flight time is only about 35 minutes1. Alternatively, Aleson Shipping Lines offers ten-hour overnight ferry trips from Zamboanga City.

2.From Jolo to Bangas Island: Once you arrive in Jolo, Bangas Island is just about 3.6 nautical miles from the port of Jolo, and it takes an average of 30 minutes to reach by boat.
