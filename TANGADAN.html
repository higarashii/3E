Tangadan Falls

Tangadan Falls is a picturesque natural attraction located in the province of La Union, Philippines. It is nestled amidst lush greenery, making it a popular destination for nature lovers and adventurers alike.

As you venture towards Tangadan Falls, you'll find yourself traversing through dense forests and scenic landscapes, creating a sense of anticipation and excitement. The journey typically involves a trek along narrow paths and crossing streams, adding an element of adventure to the experience.

Upon reaching Tangadan Falls, visitors are greeted by the sight of cascading water plummeting from a considerable height into a tranquil pool below. The falls create a mesmerizing display as the water rushes down, surrounded by towering cliffs and verdant vegetation. The pool at the base of the falls offers a refreshing respite, inviting visitors to take a dip and cool off in its crystal-clear waters.

Surrounding the falls, you'll find rocks and boulders worn smooth by the constant flow of water, providing ample opportunities for exploration and photography. Many visitors enjoy picnicking on the rocks or simply basking in the beauty of their surroundings.The ambiance at Tangadan Falls is serene and rejuvenating, making it an ideal escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. Whether you're seeking adventure, relaxation, or simply a connection with nature, Tangadan Falls offers a truly unforgettable experience for all who visit.

How to get there

To reach Tangadan Falls, you can follow these general directions:

Begin from San Juan town proper in La Union province.

Head to the jump-off point for Tangadan Falls, which is usually in the Barangay of San Gabriel.

You can hire a tricycle or motorcycle (habal-habal) from San Juan town proper to take you to the jump-off point in San Gabriel.

From the jump-off point, you'll likely need to hike or ride a rented bamboo raft to reach the falls, depending on the current arrangements for access.

Follow the designated trail or instructions provided by local guides or officials to ensure a safe journey to Tangadan Falls.
