Great Santa Cruz Island

Great Santa Cruz Island is a small inhabited island located in the southern region of the Philippines. Situated 4 kilometers, approximately 2.49 miles south of downtown Zamboanga City, boasts one of the few pink sand beaches in the world.

The Island together with Little Santa Cruz Island, is a protected area in Region 9 of the Philippines. Jointly known as the Great and Little Sta. Cruz Islands Protected Landscape & Seascape.

The color of the sand comes from the pulverized red organ pipe coral, which has eroded over eons and mixed with the white sand. Great Santa Cruz Island promises an unforgettable experience for beach lovers and nature enthusiasts alike.

How to get there

To reach Great Santa Cruz Island, you can follow these general directions:

By Public Transportation:

1. Take a bus or taxi to Paseo del Mar in Zamboanga City. From Paseo del Mar, you can hire a banca (a traditional boat) to take you to Great Santa Cruz Island. The boat ride takes about 15-20 minutes.

2. Each boat can accommodate up to 10 persons and costs Php 1,000.00 for a round trip. An entrance fee of Php 20.00 and a Php 5.00 terminal fee are collected once you arrive on the island.

By Private Vehicle:

1. If you're traveling by private vehicle, drive to Paseo del Mar in Zamboanga City and Park your vehicle securely and proceed to the boat area.

2. The boat will take you to Great Santa Cruz Island. The boat ride takes about 15-20 minutes.
