Dambana ng Kagitingan (Shrine of Valor)

The Shrine of Valor is situated atop Mount Samat, a strategic vantage point overlooking the Bataan Peninsula and Manila Bay. Mount Samat is approximately 20 kilometers from the town of Balanga, the capital of Bataan Province, and is easily accessible by car or public transportation.

The Shrine of Valor commemorates the heroism and sacrifices of Filipino and American soldiers who fought during the Battle of Bataan in World War II. The battle, which took place from January to April 1942, marked a significant chapter in Philippine history and is remembered for the courage and resilience of the defenders against overwhelming odds.

At the heart of the shrine complex is a towering cross, standing at 92 meters tall, symbolizing the Filipinos' faith and courage during the war. The cross serves as a memorial to the soldiers who perished during the battle and is visible from miles around.

Adjacent to the cross is a museum that showcases artifacts, photographs, and memorabilia related to the Battle of Bataan and World War II. Visitors can learn about the events leading up to the battle, the experiences of soldiers and civilians during the war, and the significance of the shrine in Philippine history.

How to get there

To get to the Dambana ng Kagitingan, also known as the Shrine of Valor, you can follow these general directions:

From Manila or Nearby Cities:

1. By Private Vehicle : If you have access to a car or motorcycle, you can drive to Mount Samat from Manila or nearby cities. Take the North Luzon Expressway (NLEX) and continue on the Subic-Clark-Tarlac Expressway (SCTEX) until you reach the Dinalupihan exit. From there, follow the signs to Mount Samat. The Shrine of Valor is located at the summit of Mount Samat.

2. By Public Transportation : If you prefer to use public transportation:

A. By Bus : Take a bus bound for Balanga, Bataan, from Cubao or Pasay bus terminals in Metro Manila. From Balanga, you can take a jeepney or tricycle to Mount Samat. Alternatively, some buses may have routes that pass by or stop near Mount Samat.

B. By Jeepney : From Balanga, you can take a jeepney bound for the town of Pilar, where Mount Samat is located. From Pilar, you can hire a tricycle or take a jeepney to the shrine itself.

Arrival at Mount Samat:

Once you arrive at Mount Samat, you will need to park your vehicle in the designated parking area (if you drove). Purchase tickets at the entrance gate, which grants you access to the shrine complex and museum. You can then either hike or take a shuttle to reach the summit where the Dambana ng Kagitingan (Shrine of Valor) is located. The shuttle service is usually available for visitors who prefer not to hike up the steep road to the summit.

It's advisable to check the operating hours of the shrine complex and museum before planning your visit, as they may vary.
