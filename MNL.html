Manila Ocean Park

Manila Ocean Park, nestled along the scenic Manila Bay in the Philippines, is an enchanting aquatic wonderland that invites visitors to explore the fascinating depths of the ocean without leaving the city. Spanning an expansive area, this marine-themed park offers an array of captivating attractions and experiences that cater to visitors of all ages.

As you step into Manila Ocean Park, you're greeted by a vibrant tapestry of marine life, from colorful tropical fish to majestic sharks and graceful rays. The park's main highlight is its stunning Oceanarium, one of the largest in Southeast Asia, where you can journey through a mesmerizing underwater tunnel and marvel at an impressive variety of marine species swimming overhead.

Adventure-seekers can satisfy their adrenaline cravings with attractions like the Aquanaut Voyage, where you can don a specialized helmet and walk along the ocean floor surrounded by marine life, or the thrilling Fish Spa, where tiny fish nibble away dead skin cells, leaving your feet feeling rejuvenated.

With its blend of entertainment, education, and conservation, Manila Ocean Park promises an unforgettable journey into the enchanting world beneath the waves, leaving visitors inspired and awestruck by the beauty and diversity of our oceans.

How to get there

To reach Manila Ocean Park, you can follow these general directions:

By Public Transportation:

1. If you're coming from the northern or southern part of Metro Manila, you can take the LRT Line 1 and alight at the United Nations Avenue Station.

2. If you're coming from the eastern part of Metro Manila, you can take the MRT Line 3 and alight at the Taft Avenue Station.

3. f you're coming from farther areas, such as Quezon City or Pasay, you can take a bus that plies the EDSA route and alight at the Taft Avenue or Buendia Station.

By Private Vehicle:

1. If you're traveling by private vehicle from nearby towns or cities, you can use online maps or navigation apps to guide you to Manila Ocean Park.

2. From the city center, you can navigate using maps or follow road signs directing you towards the park.
