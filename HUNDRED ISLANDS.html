Hundred Island

Located off the coast of Pangasinan in the Philippines, Hundred Islands National Park is a mesmerizing archipelago boasting over a hundred lush green islets dotting the turquoise waters of Lingayen Gulf. With its pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and abundant marine life, this natural wonderland offers a haven for adventure seekers, beach lovers, and nature enthusiasts alike. Embark on a journey to explore these picturesque islands, each with its own unique charm and allure, and discover the unparalleled beauty and serenity that Hundred Islands has to offer.

Hundred Islands National Park beckons with an irresistible allure, offering a myriad of compelling reasons to embark on an unforgettable adventure. Its pristine beaches, framed by crystal-clear waters, invite weary souls to unwind amidst breathtaking natural beauty. Whether you seek thrilling water sports, tranquil island hopping, or simply basking in the sun-kissed serenity, Hundred Islands promises an unparalleled escape.

Dive into its vibrant underwater world teeming with marine life, or hike through lush trails unveiling panoramic vistas at every turn. From thrilling adventures to moments of tranquil contemplation, Hundred Islands offers an immersive experience that captivates the senses and rejuvenates the spirit, making it a must-visit destination for all travelers seeking solace and adventure in equal measure.

How to get there

How to get there

To reach Hundred Island, you can follow these general directions:

Transportation Options: Determine your preferred mode of transportation. You can either take a bus, drive your own car, or book a private van or shuttle service.

Route via Bus: If you choose to take the bus, head to the Dau Bus Terminal in Mabalacat, Pampanga, which is accessible from Clark. You can take a jeepney or taxi from Clark to reach the terminal.

Bus Companies: Look for buses operated by Victory Liner or Dagupan Bus Company that are bound for Alaminos, Pangasinan. These buses typically have routes passing through the city of Alaminos, which is the jump-off point to Hundred Islands.

Alaminos City: Once you arrive in Alaminos City, Pangasinan, you can take a tricycle or jeepney to Lucap Wharf, which is the main jump-off point to Hundred Islands. It's about a 10 to 15-minute ride from the city proper to Lucap Wharf.

Boat to Hundred Islands: At Lucap Wharf, you'll find boats available for hire that can take you to Hundred Islands. You can choose from different tour packages depending on your preferences, including island hopping and snorkeling.

Travel Time: The travel time from Clark to Alaminos City is approximately 3 to 4 hours by bus, depending on traffic conditions. The boat ride from Lucap Wharf to Hundred Islands takes around 20 to 30 minutes.

Private Transportation: If you prefer driving your own car or booking a private van or shuttle service, you can take the North Luzon Expressway (NLEX) and follow the route towards Alaminos City, Pangasinan. From there, proceed to Lucap Wharf as mentioned earlier.

Enjoy Hundred Islands: Once you reach Hundred Islands, explore the various islands, enjoy swimming, snorkeling, and other water activities, and take in the beautiful scenery.
