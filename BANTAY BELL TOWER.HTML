Bantay Bell Tower

The Bantay Bell Tower, also known as the St. Augustine Parish Church Bell Tower, is an iconic structure that stands majestically atop a hill in Bantay, Ilocos Sur, Philippines. Constructed in 1591, the bell tower is a magnificent testament to the enduring legacy of Spanish colonial architecture in the region. Rising to a height of approximately 45 meters, the tower commands a commanding view of the surrounding landscape, offering panoramic vistas of verdant rice fields, rolling hills, and the winding Abra River below.

Built from bricks and coral stones, the Bantay Bell Tower boasts a distinctive octagonal shape and intricate architectural details that reflect the craftsmanship of its creators. Over the centuries, the tower has weathered the passage of time and witnessed countless historical events, serving as a silent witness to the rich tapestry of Ilocano culture and heritage. Its sturdy walls have withstood earthquakes, typhoons, and other natural disasters, standing as a testament to the resilience and ingenuity of the Filipino people.

As the sun sets behind the distant mountains, the Bantay Bell Tower casts a striking silhouette against the evening sky, its ancient bells chiming softly in the breeze. Illuminated by the warm glow of twilight, the tower exudes a sense of timeless beauty and tranquility, inviting visitors to step back in time and immerse themselves in the history and romance of colonial-era Philippines. Today, the Bantay Bell Tower stands as a beloved symbol of Bantay's cultural heritage, drawing pilgrims, tourists, and history enthusiasts from far and wide who come to marvel at its beauty and significance.

Visitors to the Bantay Bell Tower can climb the narrow staircase that winds its way to the top of the tower, where breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside await. From this vantage point, one can gaze out across the landscape and contemplate the rich history and heritage of Ilocos Sur, from the Spanish colonial era to the present day. Whether bathed in the golden light of dawn or silhouetted against the fiery hues of sunset, the Bantay Bell Tower continues to inspire awe and wonder in all who behold it, a timeless monument to the enduring spirit of the Filipino people.

How to get there

By Public Vehicle:

To travel from Manila to the Bantay Bell Tower in Ilocos, you can take a bus from terminals such as Victory Liner, Partas, or Florida Bus Lines.

The journey typically takes around 8-10 hours, depending on traffic and road conditions. Once you arrive in Ilocos, you can take a tricycle or taxi to reach the Bantay Bell Tower, which is located near Vigan City.

By Private Vehicle:

Car or Motorcycle

If you prefer driving, you can rent a car in Manila and take the North Luzon Expressway (NLEX) and the Subic-Clark-Tarlac Expressway (SCTEX) until you reach Ilocos.
