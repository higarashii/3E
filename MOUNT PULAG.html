Mount Pulag

Mount Pulag stands proudly as the third-highest peak in the Philippines, renowned for its stunning vistas, diverse ecosystems, and unique biodiversity. Located within the Cordillera Administrative Region (CAR) in northern Luzon, Mount Pulag is a favorite destination among nature enthusiasts and adventurers alike. Its gentle slopes are adorned with mossy forests, dwarf bamboo groves, and vibrant wildflowers, offering a kaleidoscope of colors that change with the seasons.

The highlight of any visit to Mount Pulag is the opportunity to witness the breathtaking sunrise from its summit, where the sea of clouds blankets the surrounding landscape in an ethereal glow. Beyond its natural beauty, Mount Pulag holds cultural significance for the indigenous communities of Benguet, serving as a sacred site and a symbol of ancestral heritage. Mount Pulag is situated in the municipality of Kabayan in Benguet province, approximately 2,922 meters above sea level. The most common route to reach the mountain is via land travel from Baguio City, the regional capital of CAR. From Baguio, visitors can take a bus or hire a private vehicle to reach the jump-off point in Kabayan. The journey typically takes around 4 to 5 hours, passing through scenic mountain roads and quaint rural villages.

Upon arrival in Kabayan, adventurers must register at the DENR office and secure a permit before embarking on the trek to Mount Pulag's summit. Guided tours and transportation services are available for those who prefer organized excursions. While camping is the primary accommodation option on Mount Pulag, there are also homestays and lodges available in nearby communities for those who prefer more comfortable amenities. Campsites are strategically located along the trails, offering designated areas for tents and basic facilities such as toilets and water sources.

Visitors can choose from various trekking routes, each offering unique landscapes and levels of difficulty. The most popular trails include the Ambangeg, Akiki, and Tawangan routes, each providing distinct perspectives of Mount Pulag's natural splendor. Activities on the mountain range from leisurely nature walks and birdwatching to challenging summit climbs and stargazing sessions. Regardless of one's level of experience, a journey to Mount Pulag promises an unforgettable adventure amidst the pristine wilderness of the CAR Region.

How to get there

By Air:

The nearest airport is in Baguio City, the Loakan Airport. From Baguio, you can take a bus or hire a private vehicle to the jump-off point of Mount Pulag. The travel time is approximately 4-5 hours.

By Land:

From Manila, take a bus bound for Baguio City. From Baguio City, proceed to the jump-off point of Mount Pulag by bus or private vehicle.
