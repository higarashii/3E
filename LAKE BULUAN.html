Lake Buluan

Lake Buluan is a lake located on the island of Mindanao in the Philippines. With an estimated surface area of 61.34 square kilometres (23.68 sq mi), it is the third largest lake in Mindanao,after Lake Lanao and Lake Mainit. It has an average elevation of 4.5 metres (15 ft).

The lake is sandwiched between the provinces of Maguindanao and Sultan Kudarat. The lake falls under the political jurisdiction of the municipalities of Buluan and Mangudadatu of Maguindanao and President Quirino and Lutayan in Sultan Kudarat.The lake consists of adjoining marshy basins of the Pulangi, Maanoy, Buluan, and Alah rivers, which are all tributaries of the Mindanao River.

Lake Buluan is the third largest lake in Mindanao and the sixth largest in the country. With an estimated area of 62 square kilometers, the lake is a major source of livelihood for the people living in the towns of Pandag, Mangudadatu, and Buluan in Maguindanao and the town of Lutayan in the Province of Sultan Kudarat.

Lake Buluan's main produce is tilapia as well as murrel (dalag), tank goby (bunog), and milkfish (bangus). There are also Gouramis, snakehead murrel, walking catfish and carps.

How to get there

To reach Lake Buluan, you can follow these general directions:

By Public and Private Transportation:

Fly to Cotabato, then take a taxi: This is the fastest option. You can fly from Manila (MNL) to Cotabato (CBO) and then take a taxi to Lake Buluan. The total travel time is approximately 5 hours and 48 minutes, and the cost ranges from ₱3,410 to ₱15,1801.

Car ferry and bus: For a more economical journey, you can take a car ferry from Manila to Cagayan de Oro and then a bus to Tacurong. This route takes about 39 hours and 30 minutes, with costs ranging from ₱2,391 to ₱4,172.

Fly to Davao, then take a taxi: Another option is to fly from Manila (MNL) to Davao (DVO) and then take a taxi to Lake Buluan. The travel time is around 6 hours and 43 minutes, and it may cost between ₱4,410 to ₱8,980.

Bus and ferry: You can also take a bus from Manila Cubao to Ormoc, then a bus and ferry from Ormoc to Davao City, followed by a bus from Davao City Ecoland Bus Terminal to Tacurong. This journey takes approximately 38 hours and 56 minutes, and the cost ranges from ₱3,036 to ₱4,7321.

It's recommended to verify the specific routes and transportation options based on your starting point and current travel conditions. Additionally, always exercise caution and follow local traffic regulations while traveling.
