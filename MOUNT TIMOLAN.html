Mount Timolan

Mt. Timolan is a popular hike among local mountaineers. Rising to an impressive 1177 MASL, this mountain is a tucked away in the mineral-rich town of Tigbao. Passengers taking the Zamboanga-Pagadian route would see this mountain with its subtly conical shape before reaching the end of their journey.

Mount Timolan is a semi-conical mountain in New Tuburan and Timolan in Tigbao rising to 1,177 metres (3,862 ft) above sea level. It is considered the highest peak in the province situated in the Zamboanga Cordillera mountain range that runs north to south between the provinces of Zamboanga del Norte and Zamboanga del Sur and Zamboanga Sibugay in the Mindanao peninsula of Zamboanga. Located some 20 kilometres (12 mi) inland from the provincial capital city of Pagadian just to the south of Mount Sugarloaf, the top of the mountain has a crater lake known as Lake Maragang.
