Welcome to National Capital Region

The National Capital Region (NCR), also known as Metropolitan Manila, is the political, economic, and educational heart of the Philippines. As the country’s smallest region in terms of land area, it is also the most densely populated, housing over 13 million Filipinos as of the 2020 Census. NCR is a coastal region, bordered by Central Luzon to the north, CALABARZON to the south, Laguna de Bay to the east, and Manila Bay to the west. It comprises one municipality and 16 highly urbanized cities, including the regional center, the City of Manila. The region’s population density stands at 21,765 inhabitants per square kilometer, highlighting its urban nature

The local government structure of NCR is unique in the Philippines, as it is the only region without any provinces. It includes 16 cities: Caloocan, Las Piñas, Makati, Malabon, Mandaluyong, Manila, Marikina, Muntinlupa, Navotas, Parañaque, Pasay, Pasig, Quezon City, San Juan, Taguig, and Valenzuela, as well as the lone municipality of Pateros. Quezon City is the most populous, followed by Manila and Caloocan, while San Juan is the least populous. The total number of barangays in the region is 1,710, with Manila having the highest number at 897.

Economically, NCR is a powerhouse, contributing significantly to the country’s GDP. It is home to a diverse range of industries, including manufacturing, services, and construction. The region boasts several business districts such as Makati, which is the financial center, Bonifacio Global City in Taguig, Ortigas Center spanning Mandaluyong and Pasig, and Alabang in Muntinlupa. These areas are not only business hubs but also cultural and entertainment centers, with attractions like Rizal Park, The National Museum, Manila Bay, Intramuros, and various shopping complexes. The region’s economic vibrancy is further evidenced by the presence of multinational company offices and embassies, particularly in Makati.

Welcome to Cordillera Administrative Region

The Cordillera Administrative Region (CAR) is a distinctive region in the Philippines situated in the northern part of Luzon. Comprising six provinces – Abra, Apayao, Benguet, Ifugao, Kalinga, and Mountain Province – along with the highly urbanized city of Baguio, CAR is known for its unique geography and cultural diversity. The region's total land area spans 19,422.03 square kilometers, with Baguio City serving as its regional center. CAR is renowned for its stunning mountain ranges, including the Cordillera Central, which is home to some of the highest peaks in the Philippines.

CAR's population, as of 2020, stood at 1,797,660 people, making it one of the least populous regions in the country. The region's economy is primarily agricultural, with farming and forestry being key sources of livelihood. CAR is famous for its highland vegetables, earning it the moniker "salad bowl of the Philippines." Additionally, the region is rich in mineral resources, with deposits of gold, copper, silver, and other non-metallic minerals contributing to its economic landscape. The real estate market in CAR, particularly in Baguio City, has seen growth due to its popularity as a tourist destination and a university town.

Culturally, CAR is a melting pot of indigenous communities, with the Igorot people being central to the region's heritage. The rich tapestry of languages, traditions, and festivals in CAR reflects the diverse ethnic groups that call the region home. The historical significance of CAR, marked by the creation of the region in 1987 to recognize and preserve its distinct cultures, adds to its allure. With its breathtaking landscapes, vibrant cultural scene, and economic contributions, the Cordillera Administrative Region stands as a captivating destination that seamlessly blends tradition with modernity.

Welcome to Region 2

Region 2, also known as the Cagayan Valley Region, is located in the northeastern part of Luzon, Philippines. It is composed of five provinces: Cagayan, Isabela, Nueva Vizcaya, Quirino, and Batanes. The region is known for its rich agricultural lands, diverse culture, and stunning natural landscapes. Cagayan Valley is blessed with fertile soil, making it a major agricultural hub in the country. Rice, corn, and tobacco are some of the main crops grown in the region. Fishing is also a significant industry, with the Cagayan River and its tributaries providing a bountiful catch.

The region's culture is a blend of indigenous, Spanish, and American influences. Traditional practices and rituals are still observed by many indigenous communities, adding to the region's cultural richness. The region is also known for its vibrant festivals, showcasing the colorful traditions and heritage of its people. In terms of natural attractions, Cagayan Valley boasts stunning landscapes ranging from rugged mountains to pristine beaches. The Sierra Madre mountain range runs through the eastern part of the region, offering breathtaking views and challenging trekking opportunities. The Batanes islands, on the other hand, are known for their picturesque landscapes, lighthouses, and traditional Ivatan houses.

The region's economy thrives on agriculture, with rice, corn, and tobacco as primary crops, complemented by vibrant livestock and poultry industries. Cagayan Valley is home to diverse indigenous groups, each contributing to the region's unique cultural tapestry with their languages, traditions, and folklore. From the majestic Callao Caves to the picturesque rice terraces of Nueva Vizcaya and the idyllic beaches of Batanes, the region offers a wealth of natural wonders and tourist attractions. Rich in heritage and culinary delights, Cagayan Valley invites visitors to explore its beauty and immerse themselves in its vibrant culture.

Welcome to Region 3

Region 3, officially known as Central Luzon, is an administrative region in the Philippines located in the central part of the island of Luzon. It serves as an essential hub for organizing the seven provinces that make up the vast central plains of Luzon. The region is often referred to as the "rice granary of the Philippines" due to its significant contribution to the country's rice production. Central Luzon covers a total land area of 18,230.8 square kilometers and is bordered by various regions, including Ilocos, Cordillera Administrative Region, and Cagayan Valley to the north, and the National Capital Region, Calabarzon, and Manila Bay to the south.

The seven provinces that constitute Region 3 are Aurora, Bataan, Bulacan, Nueva Ecija, Pampanga, Tarlac, and Zambales, each offering unique cultural and geographical characteristics. The region is home to bustling cities like San Fernando, Balanga, and Malolos, which are vital centers of commerce and culture. Central Luzon boasts a diverse landscape that includes towering mountains, active and extinct volcanoes, lush farmlands, and natural harbors, making it an attractive destination for tourists and a hub of economic activity.

Central Luzon is a region with a rich historical heritage, evident in its numerous historical sites, festivals, and cultural traditions. The region's economy is primarily driven by agriculture, with rice, sugarcane, and other major crops being cultivated in its fertile lands. Central Luzon's strategic location, well-connected transportation network, and vibrant cities make it a dynamic region that blends tradition with modernity, offering a mix of urban amenities and natural beauty to residents and visitors alike.

Welcome to Region IV-A

Region 4A, also known as CALABARZON, is a significant administrative region in the Philippines located in the southern part of Luzon Island. Comprised of the provinces of Cavite, Laguna, Batangas, Rizal, and Quezon, the acronym CALABARZON is derived from the first two letters of each province. This region covers a total land area of 16,873 square kilometers and is home to a population of approximately 15 million people, making it the second most populous region in the country after the National Capital Region. CALABARZON is known for its diverse economy, which includes key industries like manufacturing, construction, agriculture, and tourism.

The region is recognized for its economic vibrancy, boasting various economic zones and industrial parks that have established CALABARZON as a hub for manufacturing and export-oriented businesses. Key industries in the region encompass electronics, automotive, food processing, and garments. CALABARZON is also renowned for its tourist destinations, such as the Taal Volcano and Lake in Batangas, the Pagsanjan Falls in Laguna, and historical sites in Rizal. These attractions, coupled with its proximity to the National Capital Region, make CALABARZON a sought-after destination for both local and international tourists.

Despite its economic successes and tourist appeal, CALABARZON faces challenges like rapid urbanization, traffic congestion, and environmental degradation. Efforts are underway to address these issues and promote sustainable development in the region. With its rich cultural heritage, diverse economic activities, and natural attractions, CALABARZON stands as a pivotal region in the Philippines, embodying a blend of history, industry, and natural beauty that contributes significantly to the country's overall landscape and development.

Welcome to Region V

Welcome to Region V, Bicol Region! Nestled in the southeastern part of Luzon island, the Bicol Region, also known as Region V, is a captivating destination in the Philippines that beckons travelers with its natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and culinary delights. Comprising six provinces—Albay, Camarines Norte, Camarines Sur, Catanduanes, Masbate, and Sorsogon—this region boasts a diverse landscape ranging from majestic volcanoes to pristine beaches and lush forests.

One of the most iconic features of the Bicol Region is the imposing Mayon Volcano, often referred to as the "Perfect Cone" due to its symmetrically shaped cone. Towering over the province of Albay, Mayon Volcano is not only a geological marvel but also a sacred symbol deeply ingrained in the local folklore and mythology of the Bicolanos. Its awe-inspiring beauty attracts adventurers and nature enthusiasts from around the globe, offering opportunities for hiking, photography, and cultural immersion.

Beyond its natural wonders, the Bicol Region is steeped in a rich tapestry of culture and tradition. From the vibrant festivals like the Penafrancia Festival in Naga City to the colorful handicrafts and culinary delights such as the fiery Bicol Express dish, visitors are treated to a sensory feast that celebrates the region's unique heritage. Whether exploring centuries-old churches, indulging in spicy local cuisine, or witnessing age-old rituals, travelers are sure to be enchanted by the warmth and hospitality of the Bicolanos, making their journey through Region V an unforgettable experience.

Welcome to Region X

Northern Mindanao, also known as Region X, is a vibrant and diverse region located in the northern-central section of Mindanao in the Philippines. It is composed of five provinces: Bukidnon, Camiguin, Lanao del Norte, Misamis Occidental, and Misamis Oriental. The region boasts a population of over 5 million people as of the 2020 census, representing about 4.61% of the entire population of the Philippines. The regional center is the bustling city of Cagayan de Oro, which is one of the two highly urbanized cities in the region, the other being Iligan. The area is known for its rich cultural heritage, with a mix of indigenous tribes and a history of Spanish and American influences.

The geography of Northern Mindanao is as diverse as its culture, featuring a landscape that includes the Kitanglad Mountain Range in Bukidnon, the pristine White Island in Camiguin, and the majestic Maria Cristina Falls in Iligan. The region’s land area spans over 20,000 square kilometers, with a significant portion classified as forest land, providing a habitat for a wide range of flora and fauna. The seas surrounding the region, including the Bohol Sea and Celebes Sea, are abundant with marine life, making it an ideal location for fishing and aquaculture. The climate is generally mild and invigorating, thanks to the natural springs and high elevation areas.

Economically, Northern Mindanao is a powerhouse in the Mindanao island group, with a gross domestic product (GDP) of approximately ₱1.18 trillion or $21.2 billion as of 2023. The region’s economy is diverse, with agriculture playing a significant role, particularly in the provinces of Bukidnon and Lanao del Norte, which are known for their vast pineapple and coconut plantations. The region is also making strides in industrialization, with various manufacturing plants and processing facilities. Tourism is another key sector, with the region’s natural wonders, such as protected forests, clear mountain springs, waterfalls, white sandy beaches, coral reefs, and sheltered bays, attracting visitors from all over the world.

Welcome to BARMM

BARMM is bordered, clockwise from the Northeast, by Northern Mindanao, SOCCSKSARGEN, the Moro Gulf, Celebes Sea, and the Sulu Sea. The Bangsamoro, officially the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) is an autonomous region located in the southern Philippines. Replacing the Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (ARMM). The BARMM consists of five predominantly Muslim provinces. It is the only region in the Philippines that has its own government.

As a Philippine administrative region and part of Mindanao island group, Bangsamoro’s local government units cover 5 provinces, namely, Basilan, Lanao del Sur, Maguindanao, Sulu, and Tawi Tawi, the independent component city of Cotabato, two (2) component cities (Lamitan and Jolo, the provincial capital of Basilan and Sulu, respectively), 116 municipalities, and 2,590 barangays (including select 63 out of 67 of Cotabato province handed over expanding territorial scope as a result of the plebiscite’s second part. The regional center is the City of Cotabato).

The city of Isabela, despite being part of Basilan, and respective parent Cotabato province municipalities of added barangays are not under the administrative jurisdiction of the autonomous region.
