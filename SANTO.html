Santo Niño Diocesan Shrine

The Santo Niño Diocesan Shrine in Butuan is a spiritual haven that stands as a beacon of faith for the Catholic community. Founded in 1986, it is located along the bustling Maharlika Highway in Libertad, Butuan City, Agusan del Norte. This church is not only a place of worship but also a historical site, as it is situated in the city where many believe Ferdinand Magellan first landed in the Philippines. The shrine is dedicated to the Sto. Niño, the Holy Child Jesus, and is widely known as both the Sto. Niño Shrine and Sto. Niño Parish.It serves as a symbol of the deep religious roots and the rich cultural tapestry of the region.
The shrine’s architecture and design reflect the reverence and devotion of its congregation. As visitors step inside, they are greeted by the serene ambiance and the spiritual presence that pervades the space. The shrine is a place where the faithful can find solace and inspiration, participating in the various liturgical celebrations and community activities that take place throughout the year. It is not just a landmark of religious significance but also a cornerstone of the community, fostering unity and spiritual growth among its members.

How to get there

If you’re already in Butuan City, head towards the Libertad Public Market.
The Santo Niño Diocesan Shrine is 200 meters away from the market along the main road.
Look for it on the right side of the road before reaching the Sports Complex
