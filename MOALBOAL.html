Moalboal Beach

Moalboal Beach is a stunning coastal destination located in the town of Moalboal, Cebu. Known for its crystal-clear waters, vibrant marine life, and picturesque scenery, it's a haven for beach lovers, snorkelers, and divers. Moalboal Beach features soft white sands and clear blue waters, perfect for sunbathing, swimming, and relaxing by the shore. It's also the jump-off point for various water activities, such as snorkeling and diving. It's an ideal spot for beachgoers, offering a laid-back ambiance for sunbathing, swimming, and beach sports. Visitors can rent beachfront cottages or participate in water activities like snorkeling and diving.

Moalboal Philippines is a tiny fishing town on the western coast of Cebu Island in the Central Visayas region. It may only have a small population of around 30,000 inhabitants but this little town was one of our favourite places to stay whilst backpacking in the Philippines. Since 1970 Moalboal has evolved into a main tourist attraction due to its breathtaking underwater paradise, and has now become one of the top scuba destinations of the world. A great majority believe that Moalboal was founded by settlers coming from Bohol Island. In this connection, it has been said that one Laurente Sabanal of Bohol who was captured by the Spaniards for killing a “guardia civil” escaped from his captors, took a small boat to Southern Cebu.
