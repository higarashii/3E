Aliguay Island

Aliguay is an island in the Philippines, and a barangay of Dapitan. The island is an established marine sanctuary. The island is surrounded by white sand beaches and coral reefs. There are no water sources on the island. A few residents live on Aliguay Island, mostly selling fish to tourists.

Aliguay is a small island located about an hour away by speed boat from Dakak Beach Resort in Dapitan. Due to its remote location, only few tourists are able to visit this wonderful island.

The Aliguay Island Protected Landscape and Seascape is regarded as one of the undiscovered gems of Zamboanga. Surrounded by pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and coral reefs, this marine sanctuary has been in place for a while. Because of this, the majority of visitors to this isolated island use snorkels to view Aliguay's underwater wonders. The island is less commercialized, yet the residents welcome visitors and offer simple amenities and meals.

Though most past tourists have arrived at Aliguay by arranging rides with the local boatmen and fishermen at Dipolog and Dapitan, there isn't a set route to get to the island. Approximately Php3,000 is needed for a round-trip boat ride.

How to get there

To reach Aliguay Island, you can follow these general directions:

By Public Transportation:

1. First is booking a flight from Manila to Zamboanga International Airport. Upon arrival at Zamboanga International Airport, you’ll find public transportation options like vans or taxis within the airport.

2. These can take you to Dakak Port. From Dakak Port, you can ride a speed boat or an outrigger to reach Aliguay Island.

By Private Vehicle:

1. Travelling using a rented car or privaely own vehicle from nearby towns or cities, you can use online maps or navigation apps to guide you to Aliguay Island.

2. Renting a car is still the same as commuting. After landing at Zamboanga International Airport, drive to Dakak Port, use a navigation app to go there, and it is approximately a 2–3-hour drive and after transfer to a boat going to Aliguay Island.

It's recommended to verify the specific routes and transportation options based on your starting point and current travel conditions. Additionally, always exercise caution and follow local traffic regulations while traveling.
