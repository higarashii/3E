San Juanico Bridge

San Juanico Bridge, spanning the picturesque San Juanico Strait, stands as an iconic symbol of connectivity and progress in the Philippines. Stretching over 2.16 kilometers, it is the longest bridge in the country, with its graceful arches offering stunning vistas of the surrounding waters and landscapes. Built in the late 1960s and completed in 1973 under the leadership of former First Lady Imelda Marcos, the bridge represents a feat of Filipino engineering and ambition. Beyond its practical function as a vital transportation link between Samar and Leyte, San Juanico Bridge carries with it a rich historical significance, embodying the unity and resilience of the Filipino people and serving as a lasting testament to the country's progress and development.

Today, San Juanico Bridge continues to serve as a vital artery for the region, facilitating the movement of goods, services, and people between Samar and Leyte. Its majestic presence has made it not only a crucial infrastructure asset but also a beloved tourist attraction, drawing visitors from far and wide to admire its beauty and significance. As a testament to Filipino ingenuity and unity, San Juanico Bridge remains an enduring symbol of hope and aspiration, bridging geographical divides and fostering connections between communities in the Eastern Visayas region and beyond.


How to get there

San Juanico Bridge is accessible by land transportation from Tacloban City in Leyte and Santa Rita in Samar. Visitors can take a public utility van, bus, or private vehicle to reach either end of the bridge. From Tacloban City, it is a short drive to the bridge's Leyte side, while from Santa Rita, it is a similar distance to the Samar side. Both ends of the bridge are well-signposted, making it easy to find.
