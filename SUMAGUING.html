Samaguing Cave

Sumaguing Cave, located in Mountain Province within the Cordillera Administrative Region (CAR) of the Philippines, is a captivating destination renowned for its stunning limestone formations and underground river system. Known as the "Big Cave" among locals, Sumaguing Cave offers visitors a thrilling adventure into the depths of the earth, where they can marvel at towering stalactites and stalagmites, crystal-clear pools, and fascinating rock formations shaped by millions of years of geological processes.

Exploring Sumaguing Cave is not only an exhilarating experience but also an opportunity to witness the natural wonders that lie beneath the surface of Mountain Province. Whether you're an experienced spelunker or a first-time cave explorer, Sumaguing Cave promises an unforgettable journey into the heart of the earth. Sumaguing Cave is situated in Sagada, Mountain Province, which can be reached by bus or private vehicle from major cities such as Baguio or Manila.

From Sagada town proper, visitors can hire a local guide to lead them to the cave entrance, which is located a short distance from the town center. The cave entrance is accessible via a trekking trail, which may involve descending steep slopes and navigating rocky terrain. It is recommended to wear appropriate footwear and clothing and bring a flashlight or headlamp for illumination during the cave exploration.While there are no accommodations directly at Sumaguing Cave, visitors can find a variety of lodging options in Sagada town proper, ranging from guesthouses and homestays to hotels and inns. After a day of exploring the cave, travelers can relax and unwind in the comfort of their chosen accommodation, enjoying amenities such as comfortable beds, hot showers, and hearty meals.

In addition to exploring Sumaguing Cave, visitors to Sagada can partake in a range of outdoor activities such as trekking, hiking, and visiting other natural attractions like Echo Valley and the Sagada Hanging Coffins. Guided tours to Sumaguing Cave are available, with experienced guides providing insights into the cave's geology, history, and safety precautions for an enriching and enjoyable experience.

How to get there

By Air:

The nearest airport is in Baguio City, the Loakan Airport. From Baguio, take a bus or hire a private vehicle to Sagada. Sumaguing Cave is located near the town proper.

By Land:

Sumaguing Cave is one of the popular attractions in Sagada, easily accessible by foot or by hiring a local guide.
