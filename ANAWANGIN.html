Anawangin Cove

Located at Pundaquit, San Antonio, 2206 Zambales, Philippines

Anawangin Cove is a picturesque beach destination located in San Antonio, Zambales, Philippines. It is known for its unique landscape featuring white sand beaches, towering pine trees, and rocky cliffs.

The cove is a popular camping spot, attracting visitors who enjoy outdoor activities such as swimming, snorkeling, trekking, and beach camping. Accessible only by boat or by trekking through rugged terrain, Anawangin Cove offers a peaceful retreat away from the hustle and bustle of city life.

Visitors are advised to bring their own camping gear and supplies, as amenities are limited on the island. Additionally, it's important to practice responsible tourism and leave no trace behind to help preserve the natural beauty of the area.

How to get there

To get to Anawangin Cove, you'll typically need to travel to San Antonio, Zambales, in the Philippines.

By Water Transportation:

1. The most common way to reach Anawangin Cove is by arranging a boat transfer from Pundaquit or San Antonio.

2. These boat rides usually take around 30-45 minutes, depending on sea conditions. You can negotiate boat rentals at the local beach or through tour operators in the area. For more adventurous travelers, there's an option to hike to Anawangin Cove from Pundaquit.

By Land Transportation:

1. For more adventurous travelers, there's an option to hike to Anawangin Cove from Pundaquit. The trek takes about 2-3 hours, depending on your pace and the trail conditions.

2. It's recommended to hire a local guide if you're not familiar with the route. Whichever option you choose, it's a good idea to check the weather forecast and sea conditions before your trip, especially during the rainy season.

Additionally, make sure to pack essentials like water, snacks, sun protection, and any camping gear if you plan to stay overnight.
