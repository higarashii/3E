Buluan Island

At the capital town of Ipil, one need not to go far just to have a taste of what Zamboanga Sibugay has to offer. Located some 7 kilometers from the port of Ipil, Buluan Island is a famous beach destination among Sibugaynons and is now slowly gaining popularity in nearby towns and provinces.

Buluan Island features white sand beach and clear blue waters. The uncombed sand may not be super powdery fine but Buluan Island can offer seclusion and tranquility. The island is privately owned but no entrance fees are collected from visitors. The island is still uncommercialized. Since there are no resorts nor restaurants on the island, visitors should purchase food and drinks from the town. There are bamboo cottages and tables available to visitors at no cost. From the cottages, you can enjoy the fresh sea breeze while admiring the view of the highlands of Zamboanga Sibugay, or just head to the beach and enjoy a refreshing swim in the clear waters.

Buluan Island is also ideal for weekend overnight camping. For added safety, campers may set up their tents inside the military compound which is easily accessible from the island's Patriot Beach. There is no electricity on the island, but the military camp has solar powered generators installed used to power light bulbs and small electronic devices.

How to get there

To reach Buluan Island, you can follow these general directions:

By Public Transportation:

1. First is booking a flight from Manila to Zamboanga International Airport. Upon arrival at Zamboanga International Airport, you'll find public transportation options like vans or taxis within the airport.

2. These can take you to Ipil Port. From Ipil Port, you can ride a speed boat or an outrigger to reach Buluan Island.

By Private Vehicle:

1. Travelling using a rented car or privaely own vehicle from nearby towns or cities, you can use online maps or navigation apps to guide you to Buluan Island.

2. Renting a car is still the same as commuting. After landing at Zamboanga International Airport, drive to Ipil Port, use a navigation app to go there, and it is approximately an hour of drive and after transfer to a boat going to Buluan Island.

It's recommended to verify the specific routes and transportation options based on your starting point and current travel conditions. Additionally, always exercise caution and follow local traffic regulations while traveling.
