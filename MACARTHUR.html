MacArthur Landing Memorial National Park

MacArthur Landing Memorial National Park is a historical site located in Palo, Leyte, Philippines. It commemorates the historic landing of General Douglas MacArthur and his troops on October 20, 1944, during the liberation of the Philippines from Japanese occupation in World War II. The park features larger-than-life bronze statues depicting the momentous event, including General MacArthur wading ashore, accompanied by other key figures. Visitors can explore the park's landscaped gardens, monuments, and interpretive displays, which provide insights into the significance of the Leyte Landing in Philippine history.


How to get there

MacArthur Landing Memorial National Park is easily accessible from Tacloban City, the capital of Leyte province. Visitors can take a public utility van or jeepney from Tacloban City to Palo town proper, where the park is located. From there, it is a short walk or tricycle ride to the park entrance. Private vehicles can also reach the park via the Maharlika Highway, with parking available near the site.
