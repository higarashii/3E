Tubbataha Natural Reef

Tubbataha Reefs Natural Park lies in a unique position in the centre of the Sulu Sea, off the coast of Palawan, Philippines and includes the Tubbataha and Jessie Beazley Reefs. It protects an area of almost 100,000 hectares of high quality marine habitats containing three atolls and a large area of deep sea. The property is home to a great diversity of marine life. The reef ecosystems support over 360 species of coral and almost 700 species of fish, making it a hotspot for marine conservation and ecotourism. Tubbataha is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is highly protected to preserve its ecological significance. The reserve also protects one of the few remaining colonies of breeding seabirds in the region.

How to get there

By Flights

Puerto Princesa in Palawan is the jump-off point for travelers going to Tubbataha. Major airlines such as Philippine Airlines, Cebu Pacific and AirAsia have nonstop Manila to Puerto Princesa flights from NAIA International Airport Terminal 2 and 3. The usual travel time is an hour and a half. It's best to book flight months in advance as seats might be limited and for you to get cheap flights from Manila to Puerto Princesa, especially when traveling during peak season.

By Sea 2Go Travel offers a route from Manila to Puerto Princesa. The ferry usually departs during Tuesday and Friday and will arrive at the Puerto Princesa Pier. Travel time is almost a day. There are different types of seats, and it might get booked quickly, so make sure to reserve at least a day before your estimated date of departure.
