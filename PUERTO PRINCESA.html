Puerto Princesa, Palawan

The city is in the western Philippine province of Palawan. It is the westernmost city in the Philippines. It's known for its stunning natural attractions, including the UNESCO-listed Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park, home to an underground river. The city also boasts beautiful beaches, such as Honda Bay and Sabang Beach, and lush mangrove forests. Puerto Princesa is a popular destination for eco-tourism, offering opportunities for island hopping, snorkeling, diving, and wildlife watching. Additionally, it serves as a gateway to other attractions in Palawan, such as El Nido and Coron.

How to get there

By Flights

Major airlines such as Philippine Airlines, Cebu Pacific and AirAsia have nonstop Manila to Puerto Princesa flights from NAIA International Airport Terminal 2 and 3. The usual travel time is an hour and a half. It's best to book flight months in advance as seats might be limited and for you to get cheap flights from Manila to Puerto Princesa, especially when traveling during peak season.

By Sea

By ferry, 2Go Travel offers a route from Manila to Puerto Princesa. The ferry usually departs during Tuesday and Friday and will arrive at the Puerto Princesa Pier. Travel time is almost a day. There are different types of seats, and it might get booked quickly, so make sure to reserve at least a day before your estimated date of departure.
