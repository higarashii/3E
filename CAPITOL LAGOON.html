Capitol Lagoon Park

Capitol Lagoon Park is located at Corner Gatuslao and Lacson Streets, Provincial Capitol Complex, Bacolod City, Western Visayas. It is prominent for the sculpture of a woman standing next to a water buffalo and a man pulling another water bufallo. These sculpture made by Italian sculpture Francesco Riccardo Monti located on both ends of the Lagoon in the province of Bacolod.

In the middle of the Lagoon, there stood The Negros Occidental Provincial Capitol building which became the landmark of the place. The Park offers a lot of activities which is popular even to tourists, locals also use this place a lot to jog, dance with zumba, school dance rehearsals, and even feeding thousands of tilapias living in the Lagoon.

How to get there

To reach Capitol Lagoon Park, you can follow these general directions:

1. The fastest way to get from Manila to Bacolod–Silay Airport (BCD) is to train and fly which takes 3h 26m and costs ₱1,800 - ₱4,900. Tourists can also ride a ferry if they want a cheaper one, it costs around ₱1,600 - ₱8,000 and takes 28h 18m.

2. From the Bacolod-Silay Airport, take a van or tricycle to Silay's Highway (Rizal St.). Take a 15 kilometer ride by jeepney or bus to Bacolod City and drop by Robinson's Mall, if the jeepney doesn't go directly to the park. Take another jeepney to Lacson St. and tell the driver to drop you at the lagoon.
