Sagada Hanging Coffins

The Sagada Hanging Coffins are an ancient burial tradition that has fascinated visitors for generations. Located in the mountainous region of Sagada, Mountain Province, these hanging coffins are suspended high on limestone cliffs, a practice believed to have originated from the local Igorot people. This sacred burial site holds deep cultural significance, as it reflects the Igorot belief in the afterlife and their respect for the natural environment. The tradition of placing coffins in elevated locations is thought to bring the deceased closer to the spirits of their ancestors and ensure their souls' peaceful transition to the next life.

Today, the Sagada Hanging Coffins serve as a poignant reminder of the region's rich cultural heritage and continue to attract travelers from around the world seeking to witness this unique and awe-inspiring custom. Sagada is located in the Mountain Province of the Cordillera Administrative Region (CAR) in the northern part of the Philippines. To reach Sagada Hanging Coffins, visitors typically travel by bus or private vehicle from major cities like Manila or Baguio. From Manila, the journey takes approximately 12-14 hours by bus, passing through scenic mountain roads and picturesque landscapes.

Once in Sagada town proper, visitors can take a short hike to reach the hanging coffins site, guided by local tour operators or knowledgeable guides who can provide insights into the cultural significance of this ancient burial practice. Sagada offers a range of accommodation options to suit every traveler's needs, from cozy guesthouses and homestays to comfortable hotels and lodges. Visitors can choose to stay in the town center or opt for accommodations closer to nature, surrounded by lush forests and breathtaking views of the mountains.

In addition to exploring the Sagada Hanging Coffins, travelers can partake in a variety of activities such as cave exploration, trekking to waterfalls, and visiting ancient burial caves. Local guides are available to lead tours to other cultural sites and natural attractions, providing travelers with a deeper understanding of Sagada's rich history and vibrant culture. After a day of adventure, visitors can relax and unwind in one of Sagada's quaint cafes or restaurants, sampling local delicacies and enjoying the warm hospitality of the Igorot people.

How to get there

By Air:

The nearest airport is in Baguio City, the Loakan Airport. From Baguio, take a bus or hire a private vehicle to Sagada. The journey takes approximately 5-6 hours.

By Land:

From Manila, take a bus bound for Banaue or Baguio. Alight at Bontoc, then take another bus or van to Sagada.
