Baluarte

Baluarte is a Spanish term that translates to "bulwark" or "fortress" in English. It's commonly used in the Philippines to refer to a defensive structure, typically built during the Spanish colonial period. Baluartes were strategically positioned along the perimeter of fortresses or walled cities to provide additional defense against potential attacks.

In some cases, "baluarte" is also used to describe elevated viewpoints or lookout towers constructed for defensive purposes. These structures allowed soldiers to survey the surrounding landscape and monitor any approaching threats.

How to get there

To reach Baluarte, you can follow these general directions:

Start from Vigan City in Ilocos Sur, Philippines.

Baluarte is located in the Barangay of Tamag, which is around 10-15 minutes away from Vigan City proper.

You can hire a tricycle or take a jeepney from Vigan City to Barangay Tamag.

Inform the driver you're heading to Baluarte.

Once you arrive in Barangay Tamag, follow the signs or ask locals for directions to Baluarte.
