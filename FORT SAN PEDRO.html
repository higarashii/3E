Fort San Pedro

Fort San Pedro situated at A. Pigafetta Street, Cebu City, Philippines.

Fort San Pedro is the oldest and smallest fort in the Philippines and is one of the cityâ€™s pride. Lying in Plaza Independencia in the pier area of Cebu, the fort holds a rich history of the island and the historical events during the Spanish rule.

At present, Fort San Pedro is a historical park under the care and administration of the Cebu City government. You can visit the museum inside the fort that holds several preserved Spanish artifacts, such as paintings, sculptures, old photographs and items, and documents. The spacious lawn of the fort now serves as a perfect venue for occasions like weddings, birthdays, and others, but only with a permit from the designated personnel.

How to get there

For you to be able to reach Fort San Pedro, If you're arriving in Cebu City by plane, you'll land at Mactan-Cebu International Airport (CEB), which is located on Mactan Island. From the airport, you have several options to get to Fort San Pedro. You can easily find taxis, Grab, or Uber services at the airport. Simply exit the airport terminal and look for the designated taxi pickup area or book a Grab or Uber ride through their respective apps. Inform the driver that you want to go to Fort San Pedro in Cebu City, and they will take you there. Some hotels and resorts in Cebu City offer airport shuttle services for their guests. If you're staying at one of these accommodations, you may be able to arrange for a shuttle to take you directly to Fort San Pedro. If you prefer the flexibility of having your own transportation, you can rent a car at the airport. Several car rental companies have counters at the airport where you can arrange for a vehicle. From there, you can drive to Fort San Pedro using GPS or a map. There are also public buses that operate between the airport and various points in Cebu City. You can take a bus heading towards the city center and ask the driver to drop you off near Fort San Pedro. From there, you may need to walk a short distance to reach the fort.

Once you arrive at Fort San Pedro, you'll need to pay an entrance fee to access the site and explore its historical significance. Enjoy your visit!
