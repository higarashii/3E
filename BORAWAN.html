Borawan Beach

Borawan Beach in Padre Burgos, Quezon Province, Philippines, is renowned for its captivating beauty, blending the white sands of Boracay and the limestone cliffs of Palawan. This picturesque destination offers visitors a tranquil and scenic escape, perfect for beach lovers and nature enthusiasts seeking a serene and unspoiled tropical paradise.Borawan Beach boasts a stunning seascape characterized by powdery white sand, crystal-clear waters, towering limestone cliffs, and lush greenery, creating a postcard-perfect backdrop for relaxation and exploration.

The beach provides various activities such as swimming, snorkeling, kayaking, island hopping, beach camping, and hiking up the limestone formations, catering to those seeking both relaxation and adventure.

Borawan Beach is accessible by land and sea, with most visitors arriving by bus or private vehicle to Padre Burgos, followed by a boat ride to the beach. The journey provides scenic views of rural landscapes and coastline.Borawan Beach offers a harmonious blend of natural wonders, adventure opportunities, and tranquil retreats, making it a must-visit destination for travelers seeking a pristine beach getaway with stunning landscapes that rival the beauty of renowned beach destinations like Boracay and Palawan.

How to get there

From Manila, take a bus bound for Lucena Grand Terminal. You can find buses at various terminals such as Cubao, Buendia, or Alabang. The fare ranges from around P220 to P260, and the travel time is approximately 3-4 hours. - Once you reach Lucena Grand Terminal, transfer to another bus bound for Unisan. The fare is around P35 to P40, and the travel time is about 45-75 minutes. - Get off at QCRB Bank in Padre Burgos and take a tricycle to Aplaya, which is approximately a 15-minute ride. This is where you can find tour boats that can take you to Borawan Beach. The boat rental fee is around P1800 and is good for 8 people.
