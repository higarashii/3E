Mount Pinatubo

Mount Pinatubo is an active stratovolcano located on the island of Luzon in the Philippines, straddling the boundaries of Zambales, Tarlac, and Pampanga provinces. It is most famous for its catastrophic eruption on June 15, 1991, one of the largest volcanic eruptions of the 20th century. The eruption caused massive devastation, leading to widespread ashfall, the formation of a caldera, and the collapse of the volcano's summit. It also triggered pyroclastic flows and mudflows (lahars) that displaced thousands of people and significantly altered the surrounding landscape.

Despite the destruction caused by the 1991 eruption, Mount Pinatubo has since become a popular tourist destination. The volcano's caldera is now filled with a stunning blue-green lake, offering a breathtaking view for visitors. Trekking to the crater lake is a popular activity, requiring a combination of off-road vehicle rides and hiking. The surrounding area has been largely reforested, and the trails provide insights into the volcanic history and the region's recovery.

To visit Mount Pinatubo, the usual starting point is in Capas, Tarlac, from where you can take a guided tour. These tours typically involve a 4x4 vehicle ride through the rugged terrain of the lahar fields, leading to the jump-off point for the trek. From there, it's a hike of about 2 to 2.5 hours to reach the stunning crater lake at the summit. Many tours start early in the morning to avoid the midday heat, and it's recommended to book with a reputable tour operator for safety and proper guidance.
