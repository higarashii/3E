Carbin Reef

Located in the province of Negros Occidental, off the coast of Sagay town, is a pristine white sandbar known as the Carbin Reef. It is also included in the 32,000-hectare Sagay Marine Reserve, a protected marine area. Bring snorkeling gear when you visit the reef since, in addition to its stunning white beach, the surrounding waters are teaming with animals. A thriving aquatic habitat with colorful schools of fish and other marine life may be found beneath the waves.

A tongue-shaped sand bar formed of coral rubbles is part of the 200-hectare marine sanctuary known as Carbin Reef. Its gorgeous, dreamlike scenery makes for some Instagram-worthy photos, and its crystal clear water is ideal for water-based activities.

How to get there

To reach Carbin Reef, you can follow these general directions:

Book a flight to the closest airport to Negros Occidental, Bacolod-Silay Airport. From the airport, take a cab or public transit to the port, where boats to Carbin Reef can be accessible. Check out vessels or ferries at the harbor that provide transportation to Carbin Reef. Don't forget to ask about availability and schedules.

It's recommended to verify the specific routes and transportation options based on your starting point and current travel conditions. Additionally, always exercise caution and follow local traffic regulations while traveling.
