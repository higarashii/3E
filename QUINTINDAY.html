QUITINDAY GREENHILLS

Quitinday Green Hills in the Philippines, specifically located in Camalig, Albay. These picturesque hills are known for their stunning landscape, featuring rolling green hills and scenic views of nearby volcanoes like Mayon Volcano It's a popular spot for hikers and nature enthusiasts.

Quitinday Green Hills is not just a place for nature lovers; it's also rich in cultural and historical significance. The area is inhabited by indigenous people known as the Agta, who have lived there for centuries. Their way of life, traditions, and knowledge of the land add a unique layer to the experience of visiting Quitinday Green Hills.

One of the highlights of a visit to Quitinday Green Hills is the opportunity to hike to the top of the hills. The trek offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding landscape, including the majestic Mayon Volcano on clear days. It's a relatively easy hike suitable for all ages and fitness levels, making it a great activity for families and groups of friends.



How to get there

1.Fly to Legazpi City: If you're coming from outside the region, the nearest major airport is Legazpi Airport (also known as Bicol International Airport). You can fly to Legazpi City from Manila or other major cities in the Philippines.

2.From Legazpi City to Camalig: Once you arrive in Legazpi City, you can take a tricycle or taxi to the van or bus terminal. From there, you can catch a van or bus bound for Camalig. Camalig is approximately a 30-minute to 1-hour drive from Legazpi City, depending on traffic and road conditions.

3.From Camalig to Quitinday Green Hills: Once you reach Camalig, you can hire a tricycle or jeepney to take you to Quitinday Green Hills. It's about a 20-30 minute ride from the town center to the jump-off point for the hike to Quitinday Green Hills.

4.Hike to Quitinday Green Hills: From the jump-off point, you'll need to hike up to Quitinday Green Hills. The hike usually takes around 20-30 minutes, depending on your pace. The trail is relatively easy and well-marked, but it's always a good idea to wear appropriate footwear and bring plenty of water.
