Sta. Monica Church

Santa Monica Church or also known as Panay Church is one of the oldest stone churches in the Philippines. Its pillars and walls was built using coral stones, and its structure was inspired by European origin. It is located in Panay, near Roxas City in the province of Capiz.

Santa Monica Church is also a home to "Dakong Lingganay" which translates to 'big bell'. Dakong Lingganay is one of the largest of its kind in Asia and was made out of 70 sacks of coins. Visitors can still see The original Dakong Lingganay and is situated in the church's 5-story belfry while the replica was displayed beside the Museo de Santa Monica, that is also located in the church's compound.

The Santa Monica Church in Panay, Capiz was built in 1774 by Fr. Miguel Murguia. However, due to being heavily damaged by a typhoon that struct the province last January 17, 1875. The church was rebuilt in 1884 under the supervision of Fray Jose Beloso, OSA. The church stands until today. By the virtue of the National Historical Institute Resolution No. 3, the church was declared as a national historical landmark in 1997 and on July 31, 2001, Sta. Monica Church was declared as a national cultural treasure by the National Museum.

How to get there

To reach Sta. Monica Church, you can follow these general directions:

1. From Manila, take Cebu Pacific or Philippines Airlines to Roxas City.From either Caticlan or Kalibo, board a Ceres bus to Iloilo and get off at Sigma bus stop. The fare from Caticlan is ₱215 while the fare from Kalibo is ₱107. Hop on a Roxas-bound bus or jeep from Sigma to get to Roxas City.

2. From any point in Roxas City, you can board a tricycle to the terminal of Panay-bound trikes. The fare from the terminal to Panay is ₱15/person and it requires at least 4 passengers for it to leave.
