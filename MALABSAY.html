Malabsay Falls

Malabsay Falls is a natural wonder located in the Philippines, specifically in the province of Camarines Sur on the island of Luzon. It's a popular destination for both locals and tourists who are seeking to experience the beauty of nature. The falls cascade down from a height into a serene pool below, surrounded by lush greenery and rock formations, creating a picturesque scene. Visitors can enjoy swimming in the cool waters, hiking through the surrounding forests, and simply appreciating the tranquility of the area. Malabsay Falls offers a refreshing escape from the hustle and bustle of city life, making it an ideal spot for relaxation and adventure alike. Whether you're an avid nature enthusiast or simply looking for a peaceful retreat, Malabsay Falls has something to offer for everyone.

How to get there

To get to Malabsay Falls in Camarines Sur, Philippines, you'll likely need to travel to Naga City, which is the capital of the province. From Naga City, you can reach Malabsay Falls by following these general directions:

-From Naga City to Panicuason Hot Spring Resort: Malabsay Falls is located near Panicuason Hot Spring Resort. From Naga City, you can take a tricycle or a jeepney to get to the resort. It's a roughly 30 to 45-minute ride depending on traffic and your mode of transportation.

-From Panicuason Hot Spring Resort to Malabsay Falls: Once you arrive at Panicuason Hot Spring Resort, you can either hike or ride a habal-habal (motorcycle) to reach Malabsay Falls. The falls are approximately a 30-minute hike from the resort.

-Guides and Assistance: It's advisable to hire a local guide to help you navigate the trails leading to Malabsay Falls, especially if it's your first time visiting. They can provide valuable information about the area and ensure you reach the falls safely.

-Preparation: Make sure to wear comfortable hiking shoes and appropriate clothing for the hike. Bring along water, snacks, and any other essentials you may need for the trip.
