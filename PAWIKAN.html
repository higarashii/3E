Pawikan Conservation Center

The Pawikan Conservation Center is located in the municipality of Morong, Bataan, Philippines, along the coast of the South China Sea. It's situated in Barangay Nagbalayong, Morong, approximately 30 to 45 minutes away from the Dambana ng Kagitingan (Shrine of Valor) on Mount Samat.

The primary mission of the Pawikan Conservation Center is to protect and conserve sea turtles, particularly the pawikan species, which are considered endangered. The center aims to raise awareness about the importance of sea turtle conservation and to engage in activities that contribute to the preservation of these marine creatures and their habitats.

Visitors to the Pawikan Conservation Center can expect to learn about the life cycle of sea turtles, the threats they face, and the importance of conservation efforts. The center often offers guided tours led by knowledgeable staff or volunteers, providing insights into the work being done to protect these endangered marine species.

The Pawikan Conservation Center plays a crucial role in the conservation of sea turtles in the Philippines, raising awareness about their plight and actively working towards their protection and preservation for future generations to enjoy.

How to get there

To get to Pawikan Conservation Center, you can follow these general directions:

From Manila or Nearby Cities:

1. By Private Vehicle: If you have access to a car or motorcycle, you can drive to Morong, Bataan, from Manila or nearby cities. Take the North Luzon Expressway (NLEX) and continue towards the Subic-Clark-Tarlac Expressway (SCTEX). Take the exit towards Dinalupihan and follow the signs to Morong, Bataan. The conservation center is located in Barangay Nagbalayong.

2. By Public Transportation:

A. By Bus: Take a bus bound for Balanga, Bataan, from Cubao or Pasay bus terminals in Metro Manila. From Balanga, you can then take a jeepney or tricycle to Morong. Ask the driver or conductor to drop you off near the Pawikan Conservation Center or Barangay Nagbalayong.

B. Jeepney/Tricycle: From Balanga, you can also hire a jeepney or tricycle to take you directly to the Pawikan Conservation Center. Make sure to negotiate the fare before starting your journey.

It's advisable to check the operating hours of the Pawikan Conservation Center before planning your visit, as they may vary. Consider bringing sunscreen, water, and insect repellent, especially if you plan to spend time outdoors during your visit.
