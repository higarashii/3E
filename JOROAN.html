Joroan Church

It was established in the 16th century by Spanish missionaries. The church is known for its Baroque-style architecture and its significance as a pilgrimage site for devotees of Our Lady of Salvacion. It's considered one of the oldest churches in the Bicol Region and holds cultural and religious importance for the local community.

Joroan Church is particularly renowned for its annual feast in honor of Our Lady of Salvacion, celebrated every May 19th. The feast attracts thousands of pilgrims and devotees from different parts of the Philippines. The church's interior features intricate details and religious artworks, contributing to its cultural and historical significance. Over the centuries, Joroan Church has become not only a place of worship but also a symbol of resilience and faith for the people of Albay.

How to get there

To reach JOROAN CHURCH, you can follow these general directions:

By Public Transportation:

To reach Joroan Church by public transportation, one option is to take a bus bound for Tiwi, Albay, from the main terminals in Legazpi City. These buses typically run regularly throughout the day. Upon arriving in Tiwi, you can then hire a tricycle or jeepney to take you to Joroan Church, which is located in Barangay Joroan. The trip from Tiwi town proper to Joroan Church usually takes around 30 minutes by land transportation.

By Private Vehicle:

If traveling by private vehicle, you can drive from Legazpi City to Tiwi via the Maharlika Highway, which offers a scenic journey through the countryside. Once in Tiwi, follow the signs leading to Joroan Church, which is approximately 20 kilometers away from the town proper. The road leading to the church is well-paved and accessible by car, making it a convenient option for those with their own transportation.
