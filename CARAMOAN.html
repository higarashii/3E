Caramoan Islands

Caramoan Islands Known as the Emerging Paradise of the Pacific because of its alluring white and pink sand beaches, Caramoan is a first class municipality in the province of Camarines Sur, Philippines. It is also called the Municipality of Caramoan.

Its pristine azure waters stretch out to the Maqueda Channel and the Lagonoy Gulf. The primary attraction of the area is the scenic view of the beachscapes which is defined by unique geologic configurations, white sands and clear waters. Its beauty is further boosted by unexplored lakelets, subterranean passages and caves in small lime- stone islands (Escandor, 2010).

The name Caramoan has been officially used since 1619, the year it was named by a Spanish missionary friar, Francisco de la Cruz Y Oropesa. Fr. Oropesa penetrated the thick virgin forest of the Caramoan Peninsula and founded a small settlement in a place called Baluarte.

How to get there

To reach Caramoan Islands, you can follow these general directions:

By Public Transportation:

1. Take a bus from Manila to Naga City, which can take around 8-10 hours. From Naga City, take a van or bus to Sabang Port, which is about a 1-2 hour journey. Then, board a boat from Sabang Port to Guijalo Port in Caramoan, typically a 2-hour boat ride.

2. Finally, from Guijalo Port, you can take a tricycle or jeepney to your chosen accommodation in Caramoan.

By Private Vehicle:

1. If you are traveling by private vehicle, you can drive to Caramoan. The journey can be made from Manila or Naga City, following the same route as mentioned above for public transportation.

2. Ensure to have your vehicle in good condition and follow road safety guidelines while driving to Caramoan.

These transportation options provide a comprehensive guide on how to get to Caramoan Islands in Region 5 by both public transportation and private vehicle.
