Sumilon Island

Sumilon Island boasts crystal-clear waters, pristine beaches, and a captivating sandbar. Its natural lagoon and lake add to its allure, making it a perfect escape for nature lovers and beach enthusiasts. Sumilon Island is not only beautiful but also ecologically significant. It was designated as the first underwater protected area in the Philippines. As a marine sanctuary, it hosts a diverse range of sea species, making it a haven for snorkelers and divers. If you’re planning a visit, consider staying at the Bluewater Sumilon Island Resort. The resort embodies the Filipino concept of “AMUMA”, which means caring, nurturing, and tending. With warm hospitality and a timeless Filipino home vibe, it’s the perfect place to unwind and enjoy the island’s natural beauty.

Located at bangcogon Oslob Cebu, Philippines,Sumilon Island, situated in the southeastern part of Cebu, Philippines, is approximately 125 kilometers away from Cebu City, the province’s capital. This pristine island spans an area of about 24 hectares and is surrounded by the shimmering blue waters of the Cebu Strait1. It’s a haven of untouched natural beauty, making it a must-visit destination for tourists seeking a scenic and tranquil getaway.

Sumilon Island gets its name from the word Sumilong, which means taking shelter in the local language. The Island used to be the refuge for the fishers from torrential typhoons that are common in the Philippines, because of which it is known as Sumilon Island. Sumilon Marine Reserve is the first marine park in the Philippines.

How to get there

To reach Sumilon Island, you can follow these general directions:

By Public Transportation:

Boat Departures: The only access to the island is by boat. Boats depart daily from Mainland Bancogon in Oslob to Sumilon Island at 8:00 am, 10:00 am, 12:00 pm, and 2:00 pm. The return boat from the island to Mainland Bancogon departs at 9:00 am, 11:00 am, 1:00 pm, and 3:00 pm. From Cebu to Sumilon: To get from Cebu to Sumilon Island, first reach Oslob and then take the boat to the island following the schedule above.
