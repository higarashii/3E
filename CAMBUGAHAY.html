Cambugahay Falls

Cambugahay Falls is arguably the best waterfall on Siquijor Island that is most famous for its tiered levels, strikingly blue water, and freshwater cascades, all of which are surrounded by lush rainforest.Cambugahay Falls is one of the top things to do in Siquijor, and was possibly our favourite waterfall in the Philippines. Located at Cambugahay Falls is located in Lazi on Siquijor Island.Cambugahay Falls is a 3-tiered waterfall. with crisp and clean warm water originating from natural springs. Water from the Cambugahay Falls flows down about three kilometers downstream making numerous wonderful swimming lagoons before it discharges into the Lazi Bay.

Cambugahay Falls is a hidden gem tucked away in the lush green landscape of Siquijor. It’s crystal-clear waters and lush foliage make it a paradise for nature lovers and adventurers alike. With its unique cascading tiers, Cambugahay Falls provides an unforgettable experience for visitors. The roar of the water cascading down the tiers is mesmerizing and invigorating. The surrounding area is filled with lush greenery and fresh and clean air. The tranquil atmosphere provides a refreshing escape from the hustle and bustle of daily life.

The bottom tier of Cambugahay Falls is perfect for a dip in the cool, refreshing waters. Visitors can take a dip in the pool, or they can climb up the tiers and explore the area. There is a rope swing at the top of the falls for those looking for an adrenaline rush. The swing provides a thrilling experience and an excellent opportunity to take in the breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

The area around Cambugahay Falls is covered in lush rainforest, home to various flora and fauna. The tall trees provide ample shade, making it a pleasant place to relax and enjoy the scenery. Visitors can spot various bird species, including kingfishers, doves, and eagles. There are also a variety of butterflies and insects that call the falls home.

How to get there

To reach Cambugahay Falls you can follow these general directions:

By Public Transportation:

There are a couple of options when searching for how to get to Cambugahay Falls in Siquijor, and they are by scooter, habal habal, or tricycle.You can rent a scooter in Siquijor for as little as 250 pesos per day and make your own way over to Cambugahay Waterfalls. I also highly recommend purchasing travel insurance, especially when riding on the roads in Asia. Safety first! When you arrive in Siquijor by ferry, you’ll find that there are half a dozen scooter rental stores located at the ferry terminal which is super convenient. You can pick up a set of wheels for the time you plan to spend on Siquijor Island. If you are not confident driving a scooter, the other option is to hail down a tricycle or habal habal (motorbike taxi) along the main road in Paliton and get a ride to the falls. The cost depends on how good your bartering skills are, though for a 30-minute drive you can expect to pay around 150-200 pesos.
