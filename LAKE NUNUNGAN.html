Lake Nunungan

Lake Nunungan is a natural lake that is located inside Nunungan, Lanao del Norte, Philippines. It is among the biggest and most stunning lakes in the province. It is located at Nunungan Lake, Nunungan, Lanao del Norte.

It is famous for its blue, clear waters as well as its abundant fish and aquatic life. The lake is a significant source of income for the local population and also provides a wealth of fresh water for the nearby villages.

Also, the Lake Nunungan is a beautiful and significant component of Lanao del Norte. It's a source of pride and optimism for the local community and its inhabitants. It is a favorite place for fishing as well as other activities of recreation and is also a significant element of the economy of the province.

How to Get there

To reach Lake Nunungan, you can follow these general directions:

By Land Transport:

1. From Cagayan de Oro City, you can take a bus or a van bound for Malaybalay City, which is the capital of Bukidnon. The journey takes approximately 2-3 hours, depending on the traffic conditions and the mode of transportation.

2. Once you reach Malaybalay City, you can hire a habal-habal (a motorcycle taxi) or a tricycle to take you to Lake Nunungan. It is located in Barangay Kalasungay, which is around 8 kilometers away from the city center. You can inform the driver that you want to go to Lake Nunungan, and they will take you there.
