Bangui Wind Mills

The Bangui Wind Mills are situated along the shoreline of Bangui Bay, in the municipality of Bangui, Ilocos Norte, Philippines.
The Bangui Windmills stand as a remarkable symbol of renewable energy and sustainable development in the Philippines. Erected along the scenic coastline of Bangui, Ilocos Norte, these majestic wind turbines comprise one of Southeast Asia's largest wind farm projects. The windmills, with their towering structures and rotating blades, harness the strong coastal winds to generate clean and renewable electricity for the local community. Their graceful silhouette against the backdrop of the South China Sea has made them an iconic landmark and a must-visit destination for tourists exploring the region.

The history of the Bangui Windmills dates back to the early 2000s when the Philippine government, in collaboration with international organizations, initiated the construction of the wind farm as part of efforts to diversify the country's energy sources and reduce dependence on fossil fuels. Completed in 2005, the wind farm initially consisted of 15 wind turbines, each standing 70 meters tall. Over the years, additional turbines have been added, expanding the wind farm's capacity and contributing significantly to the region's energy grid. Today, the Bangui Windmills not only provide clean energy but also serve as a testament to the Philippines' commitment to environmental sustainability and green technology.

How to get there

From Laoag City: If you're coming from Laoag City, you can take a bus or van bound for Pagudpud or Claveria. Inform the driver that you're heading to Bangui Wind Mills, and they will drop you off at the nearest point. From there, you can hire a tricycle or walk to reach the windmills.

From Pagudpud: If you're coming from Pagudpud, you can also take a bus or van heading towards Laoag City or Claveria. Inform the driver that you want to go to Bangui Wind Mills, and they will drop you off at the appropriate location.

From other parts of Ilocos Norte: If you're already in Ilocos Norte, you can find transportation options such as buses or vans that have routes passing through or near Bangui. From there, you can follow the same process of informing the driver about your destination.
