Fort Pilar

Fort Pilar, also known as Real Fuerte de Nuestra Señora del Pilar de Zaragoza, is a 17th-century military defense fortress located in Zamboanga City, Philippines.

Originally called the Real Fuerte de San José (Royal Fort of Saint Joseph), it was built in defense against pirates and raiders from the sultans of Mindanao and Jolo.

The fort was attacked by the Dutch in 1646 and later abandoned by Spanish troops who returned to Manila to fight against the Chinese pirate Koxinga.

It symbolizes the cultural heritage of Zamboanga City and is now a regional museum of the National Museum of the Philippines. Fort Pilar is now an outdoor Roman Catholic Marian shrine.
