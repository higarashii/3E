Apo Reef

Apo Reef is the largest coral reef in the Philippines! It spreads over an area of 34 square kilometers, making it the second largest connecting coral reef in the world (the first place being occupied by the Great Barrier Reef of Australia). The Apo Reef, also considered as the second largest atoll-like reef in the Philippines comprises of two isolated coral reefs that are disconnected by a 30-meter deep channel.

The clear blue waters of the channel are teeming with 285 species of colorful marine life, including tropical aquarium fish, snappers and the crevice-dwelling moray. It is one best dive spots in the world, attracting hordes of divers all the year round. The soft white sand and patches of fine corals, clearly visible through the crystal blue waters, is truly an entrancing sight to behold!

How to get there

There is no direct connection from Palawan to Apo Reef. However, you can take the taxi to El Nido airport, fly to Francisco B. Reyes, then take the travel to Apo Reef. Alternatively, you can take the Minivan to El Nido, take the walk to El Nido, Palawan, take the ferry to Coron, then take the travel to Apo Reef.
