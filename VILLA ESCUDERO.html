Villa Escudero Plantations And Resort

Villa Escudero Plantation is located approximately 90 kilometers south of Metro Manila at the provincial boundaries of Laguna and Tiaong, Quezon. A self-contained working coconut plantation and resort nestled in the heart of Tiaong, Quezon, Philippines. Established in the 1880s by the Escudero family and opened to the public in 1981, this picturesque locale offers a unique and immersive cultural experience. The sprawling estate is filled with lush greenery, coconut trees, and tranquil waters, providing a serene escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. The resort is also home to the Philippines' first hydroelectric plant, Labasin Dam, which now serves as one of the resort's most iconic attractions.

One of the highlights of Villa Escudero is the Waterfall Restaurant where guests can enjoy a sumptuous meal by the cascading waters of the Labasin Dam. The resort also offers a museum that houses an extensive collection of historic, religious, and cultural artifacts. Guests can also partake in numerous activities such as bamboo rafting, bird watching, and rural village tours. Villa Escudero Plantations and Resort is not just a place to stay, but a cultural journey that offers a glimpse into the rich history and heritage of the Philippines.
