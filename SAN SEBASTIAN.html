San Sebastian Cathedral

The original San Sebastian Church was a small wooden church with a roof made of galvanized iron that was constructed in 1825. The San Sebastian Cathedral is a prominent landmark located in Bacolod City, Philippines. This Roman Catholic cathedral is known for its stunning architectural design, featuring intricate details and Gothic-inspired elements. The small building included a single, medium-sized bell.

As a significant religious and cultural site in Bacolod City, the San Sebastian Cathedral serves as a place of worship, a symbol of faith, and a must-visit destination for tourists exploring the city's rich history and heritage.

In the seventeenth century, early Christian missionaries entrusted St. Sebastian with the protection of a little Malayan village known as Magsungay. Bernardo de los Santos, the village's first gobernadorcillo, was placed in charge of this settlement, which subsequently became known as San Sebastian de Magsungay.
