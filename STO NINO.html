Sto. Nino Shrine and Heritage Museum

Sto. Niño Shrine and Heritage Museum is a cultural and historical landmark located in Tacloban City, Leyte, Philippines. It is a sprawling complex that consists of a shrine dedicated to the Holy Child Jesus (Sto. Niño) and a museum showcasing the rich heritage and history of the region. The shrine features intricate architecture and beautiful interiors, with ornate furnishings and religious artworks. Visitors can explore the museum's exhibits, which include artifacts, photographs, and memorabilia related to the life and legacy of former Philippine First Lady Imelda Marcos, who commissioned the construction of the shrine. The site offers insights into the Marcos family's influence and contributions to Philippine society, as well as the region's cultural heritage.

How to get there

Sto. Niño Shrine and Heritage Museum is located along Real Street in Tacloban City, making it easily accessible by land transportation. Visitors can take a public utility van, jeepney, or tricycle to reach the shrine from various parts of Tacloban City. The site is centrally located and well-known among locals, so transportation should be readily available. Sto. Niño Shrine and Heritage Museum is also a popular stop for guided city tours in Tacloban.
