Casaroro Falls

Casaroro Falls is located at Taft St, Valencia, Negros Oriental, Philippines. It is in the mountainous region of Valencia, which is on the eastern side of Negros Island. The surrounding forest is also home to a variety of butterflies and tropical plants that will really amaze you. Maps and signages are well maintained and will help you step by step to discover this amazing gem of Negros Oriental.

At present, The Casaroro Falls in Valencia is one of the most featured tourist spot in the nearby city of Dumaguete. This natural tourist spot has won many hearts of travels despite the steep and rather challenging means of getting there. The refreshing waters of the Casaroro Falls strikes 30 meters down to a basin that then flows strongly along a stream. Matched with huge boulders and forestry plants, the terrain sight of the majestic falls creates a picture-perfect place to visit.

How to get there

If you're coming from Dumaguete City, the capital of Negros Oriental, you can take a private vehicle, rent a motorcycle, or hire a tricycle to reach Casaroro Falls. It's approximately a 30-40 minute drive from Dumaguete City to the jump-off point for the falls. If you prefer to use public transportation, you can take a jeepney or bus from Dumaguete City to Valencia town. From there, you can hire a motorcycle or tricycle to take you to Casaroro Falls. Alternatively, you can ask locals for directions to the falls and walk from Valencia town, although this may take longer. If you have a rental car, you can drive from Dumaguete City to Valencia and then follow the signs to Casaroro Falls. The roads in Negros Oriental are generally in good condition, making it relatively easy to navigate to the falls.

Once you arrive at the jump-off point for Casaroro Falls, you'll need to hike down a series of steep steps and pathways to reach the waterfall itself.
