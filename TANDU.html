Tandu Beach

Tan duh Beach, often referred to as Tandu Beach, is a beautiful destination located in Parang, Sulu, in the Mindanao region of the Philippines. It’s known for its powdery white sand and serene atmosphere, offering a tropical paradise away from the more tourist-heavy spots. The beach is open to the public, and visitors can rent cottages during their stay

Tandu Beach is another must-see paradise boasting of white sandy beaches fringed by lush coconut groves. The turquoise waters add to the excitement. Despite its unspoiled beauty, it remains relatively unknown and only a few tourists come here every year.

Tandu Beach is part of the Sulu archipelago, an area that has been less traveled due to safety concerns, but it holds some of the most unspoiled beaches in the country. It’s important for travelers to coordinate with the Sulu Provincial Tourism Office for arrangements, including tour guides and police escorts, to ensure a safe and enjoyable visit.

How to get there

To reach Sulu Provincial Capitol, you can follow these general directions:

By Public Transportation:

1.Travel to Zamboanga City: This city is the usual gateway to Jolo, the capital city of Sulu. From there, you can catch a ferry to Jolo

2.Buy Ferry Tickets in Advance: Purchase your ferry tickets as soon as you arrive at Zamboanga airport to secure a slot. Aleson and Montenegro Lines offer daily schedules from Zamboanga to Jolo, with a travel time of 8-9 hours. It’s recommended to take the last trip at 8 pm to save on accommodation costs, as you’ll spend the night on the ferry.

3.Follow Local Protocols: Upon arrival in Jolo, wait for your tour guide before exiting the ferry. Respect and follow the instructions of your guide and police escort for your safety.
