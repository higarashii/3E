Patar Beach

Patar Beach is a stunning stretch of coastline located in Bolinao, Pangasinan, Philippines, renowned for its pristine white sand and crystal-clear waters. Set against a backdrop of towering cliffs and lush greenery, this picturesque beach offers visitors a tranquil and scenic retreat away from the hustle and bustle of city life. The soft powdery sand and gentle waves make it an ideal destination for swimming, sunbathing, and beachcombing. Patar Beach is also known for its captivating sunsets, where vibrant hues paint the sky, creating a mesmerizing spectacle for onlookers. With its natural beauty and serene ambiance, Patar Beach is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking relaxation and rejuvenation amidst nature's splendor.

Patar Beach has been a beloved destination for locals and tourists alike for many years. Its name is derived from the word "patar," which means "white" in the local dialect, aptly describing the beach's pristine sands. Historically, Patar Beach was once a secluded fishing village, with its shores frequented by local fishermen who relied on the abundant marine life for their livelihood. Over time, as word spread about the beach's beauty, it began to attract visitors from far and wide, leading to the development of small resorts and accommodations along the coastline. Today, Patar Beach has become a popular tourist destination in Pangasinan, offering visitors a tranquil escape and a chance to experience the natural splendor of the Philippines' coastline.

How to get there

How to get there

From Manila: If you're coming from Manila, you can take a bus bound for Bolinao. Several bus companies offer trips to Bolinao from major bus terminals in Manila such as Cubao or Pasay. The bus ride takes approximately 5 to 6 hours depending on traffic.

From Alaminos City: If you're already in Alaminos City, Pangasinan (famous for the Hundred Islands), you can take a bus or van bound for Bolinao. The travel time is around 1 to 1.5 hours.
