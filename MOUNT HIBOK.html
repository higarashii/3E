Mount hibok-hibok

Located in the northern section of Camiguin Island, in the Visayas area of the Philippines, stands the active volcano known as Mount Hibok-Hibok. It is the island's tallest mountain, rising to a height of 1,332 meters (4,370 feet) and providing breathtaking sweeping views of the surrounding countryside, including the Bohol Sea and other islands. mount Hibok-Hibok is not just beautiful to look at.

it has historical significance as well It has erupted multiple times in the last century, with the most catastrophic one taking place in 1951 hundreds of people died as a result of this eruption, which also severely damaged the island's settlements and infrastructure authorities have been keeping a careful eye on the volcano ever since to make sure that both locals and tourists are safe.

Mount Hibok-Hibok is popular among hikers because of its difficult paths and varied ecosystems adventurers may expect an exhilarating experience amid the island's natural splendor as they navigate through dense forests, steep slopes, and rocky terrain on the trek to the summit. Hikers may come see distinctive wildlife and plants along the route, which heightens the experience's appeal.

How to get there

To reach mount hibok-hibok, you can follow these general directions:

Travelers usually arrive at Camiguin Island by plane or boat in order to reach Mount Hibok-Hibok major Philippine cities have flight connections to the island Camiguin Airport (also called Mambajao Airport) is the closest airport as an alternative, frequent services are provided to Camiguin by boats and ferries from neighboring ports like Cagayan de Oro and Bohol.

After arriving on Camiguin Island, tourists can make travel arrangements to Barangay Ardent, which serves as the trailhead for Mount Hibok-Hibok.

The primary center of the island, the town of Mambajao, offers a variety of rental automobiles and tricycles for transportation needs.

Hikers can start their trek to the peak of Mount Hibok-Hibok from Barangay Ardent by staying on designated trails and consulting with local guides who are knowledgeable about the area and safety precautions.
