Kapurpurawan Rock Formation

Kapurpurawan Rock Formation, nestled along the coast of Burgos in the province of Ilocos Norte, Philippines, stands as a testament to the awe-inspiring forces of nature. Its name, derived from the Ilocano word "puraw," meaning white, perfectly encapsulates its most striking feature the brilliant white limestone cliffs that dominate the landscape.

Upon reaching Kapurpurawan, visitors are greeted by a scene of unparalleled splendor. The sheer grandeur of the towering cliffs, coupled with the serenity of the surrounding seascape, leaves an indelible impression on all who behold it. Many take the opportunity to explore the nooks and crannies of the rock formation, marveling at the intricate patterns and textures etched into the limestone surface.

For photographers, Kapurpurawan offers endless opportunities to capture the beauty of nature in its purest form, with each angle revealing a new perspective on this remarkable landscape. Beyond its aesthetic appeal, Kapurpurawan Rock Formation serves as a testament to the enduring power of geological processes. Scientists and researchers are drawn to this site to study its geological makeup and unravel the mysteries of its formation.

Through their investigations, they gain insights into the complex interplay of natural forces that have shaped the Earth's surface over millennia. As a cherished natural treasure and a symbol of the Philippines' rich natural heritage, Kapurpurawan continues to inspire wonder and admiration in all who visit, reminding us of the beauty and resilience of the natural world.

How to get there

By Public Vehicle:

The rock formation is about 3 kilometers from the main highway, so you need to walk along a road that is ragged or only to go horseback riding to see it close up.

Find your way to Laoag, Northern Ilocos and then pick or ride from there to Burgos or Pagudpud.

Ask the driver or driver to drop you off on the road to Kapurpurawan near Burgos City. The Laoag travel time is about 1.5 hours.

By Private Vehicle:

Car or Motorcycle

If you prefer driving, you can rent a car in Manila and take the North Luzon Expressway (NLEX) and the Subic-Clark-Tarlac Expressway (SCTEX) until you reach Ilocos.
