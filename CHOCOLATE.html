Chocolate Hills

The Chocolate Hills, is located in Brgy. Buenos Aires in the town of Carmen in Bohol, Philippines. This are a famous tourist attraction in the Philippines and a source of pride for locals. They are a unique geological formation consisting of at least 1,260 individual hills scattered across the municipalities of Carmen, Batuan, and Sagbayan in Bohol. The hills are mostly conical in shape and vary in size, but are generally uniform in shape. They are composed of Late Pliocene to Early Pleistocene, thin to medium bedded, sandy to rubbly marine limestone. These limestones contain the abundant fossils of shallow marine foraminifera, coral, mollusks, and algae.

What makes the Chocolate Hills particularly fascinating is their transformation during the dry season. When the rains recede, the lush green grass that covers the hills dries up and turns a chocolate brown, giving the hills their name. From a distance, they look like giant chocolate kisses scattered across the landscape. This unusual and eye-catching sight draws tourists from around the world, making the Chocolate Hills one of the most popular attractions in the Philippines. Whether you're a geology enthusiast, a nature lover, or simply someone looking for a unique travel experience, the Chocolate Hills are a must-visit.

How to get there

To reach Chocolate Hills, you can follow these general directions:

To visit the Chocolate Hills in the Philippines, start by traveling to the island of Bohol. You can fly to Tagbilaran Airport and then hire a tricycle, taxi, or rent a car to reach the town of Carmen, where the Chocolate Hills are located. It's about a 1.5 to 2-hour drive from Tagbilaran. Once you arrive in Carmen, head to the Chocolate Hills Complex, the main viewing area. From the viewing deck, you can admire the breathtaking panoramic view of the over 1,000 brown hills that resemble chocolate during the dry season. For more adventure, you can also explore the Chocolate Hills Adventure Park, where you can try zip-lining, ATV rides, and bike rentals. Make sure to check local regulations and guidelines before your visit. Enjoy the natural beauty of the Chocolate Hills.
