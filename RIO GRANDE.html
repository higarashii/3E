Rio Grande de Mindanao

The Rio Grande de Mindanao, also known as the Mindanao River, is the second-largest river system in the Philippines. Located on the southern island of Mindanao, with a total drainage area of 23,169 km2 (8,946 sq mi),[2] draining the majority of the central and eastern portion of the island, and a total length of approximately 373 km (232 mi).[1][3] It is an important transportation artery, used mainly in transporting agricultural products and, formerly, timber.

The Rio Grande de Mindanao has its source in the Central Mindanao Highlands near the northern coast of the island, specifically on the northeastern part of the province of Bukidnon, where it is known as the Pulangi River. It then flows southward across the Bukidnon Plateau, fed up by its tributaries along the way and then emerges onto the Cotabato plains, depositing fertile mountain silt as it widens and arcs westward through the Cotabato River Basin. It finally empties into Illana Bay at its mouth at Cotabato City.

Its headwaters are in the mountains of Impasugong, Bukidnon, south of Gingoog in Misamis Oriental, where it is called the Pulangi River. Joining the Kabacan River, it becomes the Mindanao River. Flowing out of the mountains, it forms the center of a broad, fertile plain in the southcentral portion of the island. Before its mouth in Illana Bay, it splits into two parallel sections, the Cotabato and Tamontaka, separated by a 180 m (590 ft) hill.

How to get there

1.By Air: The nearest major airport is Rio de Janeiro-Galeão International Airport (GIG). From there, you can take a flight to one of the airports near the river. Butuan Airport (BXU): Fly from Manila (MNL) to Butuan (BXU), then take a taxi to your destination. Laguindingan Airport (CGY): Take the train from Doroteo Jose LRT to Edsa LRT, then fly from Manila (MNL) to Laguindingan (CGY). Afterward, take a bus and a taxi to reach your destination. Ozamis Airport (OZC): Fly from Manila (MNL) to Ozamis (OZC), then take a local ferry and drive to your destination. Davao Airport (DVO): Fly from Manila (MNL) to Davao (DVO), then take a bus to your destination. Cotabato Airport (CBO): Fly from Manila (MNL) to Cotabato (CBO), then take a bus to Kalilangan, Bukidnon. Pagadian Airport (PAG): Fly from Manila (MNL) to Pagadian (PAG), then take a taxi to your destination.
2.. By Bus and Ferry: Take a bus from Manila Cubao to Tacloban, then another bus from Tacloban to Davao City. Finally, take a bus from Davao City to Valencia.
3. By Ferry and Taxi: Take a ferry from Manila to Cagayan de Oro, then a taxi to Valencia.
