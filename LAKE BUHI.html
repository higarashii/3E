Lake Buhi

Lake Buhi is not just a scenic spot but also holds cultural significance for the Buhinon people, the indigenous community living around the lake. They have a deep connection to the lake and its ecosystem, which is reflected in their traditions, folklore, and livelihoods. The lake is also a designated protected area to conserve its biodiversity and ensure sustainable fishing practices. Visitors can learn about the local culture and environmental conservation efforts through community-led tours and activities.

Lake Buhi is a freshwater lake located in the province of Camarines Sur, Philippines. It's famous for being the home of the world's smallest commercially harvested fish species, the "sinarapan" or "tabios." The lake is surrounded by lush greenery and offers picturesque views of the surrounding mountains. Visitors can enjoy activities such as fishing, boating, and birdwatching while taking in the natural beauty of the area. Additionally, there are resorts and accommodations available nearby for those looking to extend their stay and explore more of the region.

Lake Buhi's unique ecosystem is home not only to the sinarapan but also to a variety of other aquatic species, including tilapia and carp. The lake's waters support diverse flora and fauna, making it an important ecological site in the region. Efforts to conserve and protect Lake Buhi's biodiversity are ongoing, with initiatives focusing on sustainable resource management and environmental education. Visitors can participate in eco-tours and learn about the lake's ecological importance while enjoying its natural beauty and recreational activities.

How to get there

To reach Lake Buhi, you can follow these general directions:

By Public Transportation:

If you're coming from Naga City, you can take a bus or hire a private vehicle to travel to Buhi town, where Lake Buhi is located. The journey usually takes around 2 to 3 hours, depending on traffic and road conditions.

By Private Vehicle:

If traveling by private vehicle, you can drive from Legazpi City to Naga City via the Maharlika Highway, which offers a scenic journey through the countryside. Once in Tiwi, follow the signs leading to Lake Buhi, which is approximately 20 kilometers away from the town proper. The road leading to the lake is well-paved and accessible by car, making it a convenient option for those with their own transportation.
