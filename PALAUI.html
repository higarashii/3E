Palaui Island in Cagayan

Nestled in the northernmost part of the Philippines, Palaui Island emerges as a pristine gem in the province of Cagayan. Blessed with unspoiled natural beauty, this rugged paradise offers a captivating blend of lush greenery, dramatic cliffs, and secluded beaches kissed by crystal-clear waters. As part of the Cagayan Special Economic Zone and Freeport, Palaui Island remains relatively untouched by commercialization, preserving its ecological integrity and inviting travelers seeking an authentic escape from the bustling city life.

Beyond its breathtaking landscapes, Palaui Island boasts a rich historical heritage and cultural significance. It served as a filming location for the reality TV show Survivor, further adding to its allure and mystique. The island is also home to Cape Engaño Lighthouse, a historic Spanish-era beacon perched atop a hill, offering panoramic views of the surrounding seascape. Visitors can immerse themselves in the island’s past while exploring ancient settlements and archaeological sites scattered throughout its rugged terrain.

For adventure enthusiasts and nature lovers alike, Palaui Island presents a playground of exploration and discovery. From trekking through lush forests to diving in vibrant coral reefs teeming with marine life, the island offers a plethora of outdoor activities to satisfy every traveler’s craving for adventure. Whether hiking to the summit of Mount Palaui for breathtaking vistas or simply unwinding on its pristine shores, Palaui Island beckons with its untouched beauty and promises an unforgettable journey off the beaten path.

How to get there

To reach Palaui Island in Cagayan, you can follow these general directions:

To reach Palaui Island in Cagayan from Manila, travelers typically have two main options: by air or by land and sea.

By air: The fastest and most convenient way to get to Cagayan Province from Manila is by taking a domestic flight to Tuguegarao Airport. Several airlines operate daily flights from Manila’s Ninoy Aquino International Airport to Tuguegarao Airport. From Tuguegarao, travelers can then take a bus or private transportation to Santa Ana, the jump-off point to Palaui Island.

By land and sea: Alternatively, travelers can opt for a combination of land and sea travel. From Manila, take a bus bound for Tuguegarao City, which is approximately an 11 to 12-hour journey. Upon reaching Tuguegarao, proceed to the van terminal or bus terminal to catch a ride to Santa Ana. Once in Santa Ana, visitors can hire a boat to take them to Palaui Island.

Regardless of the chosen route, it’s advisable to check the latest travel advisories and transportation schedules before embarking on the journey. Additionally, arranging accommodations and boat transfers in advance can help ensure a smooth and hassle-free trip to Palaui Island.
