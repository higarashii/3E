Ditumabo Mother Falls

Ditumabo Mother Falls, also known as Mother Falls, is a waterfall located in the municipality of Banaue in Ifugao Province, Philippines. It is part of the Ditumabo Natural Park and is considered one of the most beautiful waterfalls in the country.

The formation of Ditumabo Mother Falls is attributed to the movement of tectonic plates that created a unique geological landscape. The falls are situated within a limestone cave system, and the water flows through an underground river before emerging from the cave entrance as a stunning waterfall.

Ditumabo Mother Falls stands at approximately 20 meters (66 feet) high and has a cascading width of about 30 meters (100 feet). The falls are surrounded by lush greenery and offer breathtaking views, making it an attractive destination for tourists.

Visitors can enjoy various activities at Ditumabo Mother Falls, including hiking to reach different vantage points for better views, swimming in natural pools near the base of the falls, taking photographs to capture its beauty, and exploring nearby attractions like rice terraces and local villages.

How to get there

To reach Ditumabo Mother Falls, you can follow these general directions:

Ditumabo Mother Falls is located in the municipality of San Luis, which is about 15 to 20 minutes away from Baler town proper. If you are starting from Baler, the most common way to get to San Luis is by renting a tricycle, motorcycle, or a private vehicle.

From San Luis, you need to continue towards Barangay Ditumabo. The road leading to the barangay where the trail starts is mostly paved but can be narrow and steep in places. If you are driving a car, make sure it’s capable of handling rough terrain, especially during the rainy season.

Upon arriving in Barangay Ditumabo, there is a designated parking area for visitors. You will need to register at the local tourism office and pay an environmental fee. This fee helps with the maintenance of the trail and the surrounding area.

From the parking area, you'll embark on a hike to the falls. The trek typically takes about 30 to 45 minutes depending on your pace. The path can be rocky and slippery, especially after rain, so wearing appropriate footwear is recommended. The trail involves crossing streams and walking through lush forest scenery.

After the hike, you will reach the stunning Ditumabo Mother Falls, which stands approximately 140 feet tall. The falls offer a refreshing and picturesque spot, perfect for swimming and enjoying the natural beauty.

Visiting Ditumabo Mother Falls is a rewarding experience for those who enjoy nature and a bit of adventure. Always be prepared with water, snacks, and other essentials for an enjoyable trek.
