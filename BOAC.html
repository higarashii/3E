Boac Cathedral

Boac Cathedral, officially known as the Cathedral Parish of the Immaculate Conception, is a prominent religious landmark in Boac, the capital of Marinduque province in the Philippines. This majestic church, built in the late 18th century, showcases a captivating blend of Spanish colonial and neo-Romanesque architectural styles. Its facade, adorned with intricate carvings and details, stands as a testament to the rich cultural heritage of the region. The cathedral's imposing structure, with its prominent bell towers and graceful archways, provides a serene and awe-inspiring atmosphere for visitors and worshippers alike.

The history of Boac Cathedral dates back to 1792 when the Augustinian friars initiated its construction. Over the centuries, the cathedral has undergone various renovations and improvements, preserving its historical and spiritual significance. Boac Cathedral served as a vital center for religious activities and community gatherings, witnessing the unfolding of Marinduque's history. It remains a symbol of enduring faith and cultural heritage, attracting visitors with its architectural grandeur and offering a glimpse into the religious and historical tapestry of the region.

How to get there

VIA AIR
- Marinduque Airport in Gasan has finally re-opened, making the trip to Marinduque from Manila faster. The most convenient way is by flying via Cebu Pacific Airlines to Marinduque, that has 3 scheduled flights weekly – Monday, Wednesday, and Saturday. The flight departs from NAIA Terminal 4, and travel time is about 45 minutes.

HOW TO GET FROM MARINDUQUE AIRPORT TO BOAC (MARINDUQUE TOWN CENTER):

By Tricycle: From Marinduque Airport, you can rent a tricycle to Boac. Fare is ₱150 ($2.90), good for 2 persons.
By Jeepney: From Gasan, you can ride a jeepney to Boac, fare is ₱25 ($0.48) per person.

HOW TO GET TO BOAC CHURCH:

If you’re already in Boac town, walk or ride a tricycle to Boac Church. Fare is ₱13 ($0.25).

The Boac Hotel

Information:

For those who long for the feeling of being at home again, the only place to be is at The Boac Hotel, the first and oldest hotel located in the heart of the town of Boac. It was founded in 1967, fronting the Archbishop Palace of the Diocese of Boac. A one-stop shop to find your souvenir, best pasalubong and finest delicacies in the province. It is also known as the famous hotel when it comes to serving native foods. Truly one of Marinduque's exceptional landmarks.
