Laswitan Falls and Lagoon

One of the unique features of Laswitan is its "tidal wave" phenomenon, which occurs during high tide. As waves crash against the rocks, water surges upward through narrow openings, creating an awe-inspiring display reminiscent of a natural fountain. Visitors can witness this breathtaking spectacle up close, feeling the cool mist against their skin as they marvel at the power of nature.

The lagoon at the base of the falls invites you to take a refreshing dip in its rejuvenating waters. Whether you're swimming, snorkeling, or simply lounging on the nearby rocks, the tranquil ambiance of Laswitan provides the perfect setting for relaxation and contemplation.

Surrounded by the beauty of Mother Nature, Laswitan Falls and Lagoon offers a serene retreat for those seeking solace and adventure alike. Whether you're a nature enthusiast, an avid photographer, or simply looking to escape the hustle and bustle of everyday life, this hidden paradise is sure to leave a lasting impression on your heart and soul.

How to get there

To reach Laswitan Falls and Lagoon, you can follow these general directions:

By Public Transportation:

1. Take a flight from Manila to Butuan City, Agusan del Norte. Butuan City is the nearest major city with an airport to Surigao del Sur. From Butuan City Airport, you can take a taxi or shuttle service to the Butuan Integrated Bus Terminal.

2. From the bus terminal, board a bus bound for Tandag City, Surigao del Sur. The bus ride typically takes around 4 to 5 hours. Upon reaching Tandag City, you can follow the instructions mentioned earlier for public transportation from Tandag City to Laswitan Falls and Lagoon.

By Private Vehicle:

1.Start by driving southward from Manila on the South Luzon Expressway (SLEX). Continue onto the Pan-Philippine Highway (also known as Maharlika Highway) passing through several provinces including Quezon, Camarines Norte, Camarines Sur, Albay, and Sorsogon. Keep driving until you reach the Matnog Ferry Terminal in Sorsogon. Board the ferry to Allen, Northern Samar.

2.From Allen, Northern Samar, drive southward through Samar Island, crossing the San Juanico Bridge to Leyte. Continue driving through Leyte until you reach Surigao del Sur. Follow signs leading to Tandag City, the capital of Surigao del Sur. From Tandag City, follow the route mentioned earlier for private vehicles from Tandag City to Laswitan Falls and Lagoon.
