Temple of Leah

The Temple of Leah is located in Cebu City, Philippines. It is situated along Gen. Maxilom Avenue, near the Cebu Taoist Temple. The temple showcases beautiful architecture and ornate decorations, making it a popular tourist attraction in Cebu City.

The Temple of Leah is a stunning Chinese temple built in honor of Leah Villa-Acosta, the wife of Chito Villa, a prominent Filipino businessman and philanthropist.

The temple features intricate carvings, colorful murals, and ornate furnishings that reflect Chinese culture and traditions. Visitors can marvel at the temple's grandeur and learn about its significance while enjoying the peaceful atmosphere it provides.

The Temple of Leah has become an iconic landmark in Cebu City, attracting both locals and tourists who come to admire its beauty and pay their respects to Leah Villa-Acosta's memory.

How to get there

To reach Temple of Leah, you can follow these general directions:

By Car: From the North Reclamation Area in Cebu City, head south on Gen. Maxilom Avenue towards Osmeña Boulevard. Continue on Gen. Maxilom Avenue for approximately 2 kilometers. The Temple of Leah will be on your left.

By Public Transportation: Take a jeepney or bus with a destination sign reading “Gen. Maxilom Avenue” or “Cebu Taoist Temple.” Alight at the stop near Gen. Maxilom Avenue. Walk north along Gen. Maxilom Avenue for about 10 minutes until you reach the Temple of Leah on your left.

By Ride-hailing Apps: Use ride-hailing apps such as Grab or Go-Van to reach the Temple of Leah from your desired location within Cebu City. Enter “Temple of Leah” as your destination when booking your ride.
