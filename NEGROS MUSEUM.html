The Negros Museum

The Negros Museum located in Gatuslao corner Lacson Street, Bacolod, Negros Occidental, Philippines, is a cultural gem housed in a beautifully restored Spanish colonial-era building.

It invites guests on a mesmerizing exploration of Negros Island's diverse history, artistic expressions, and cultural heritage, featuring a wide array of exhibits spanning from ancient artifacts to modern artworks. Through its captivating displays and informative presentations, the museum offers a profound understanding of Negros's distinct identity and historical significance.

The Negros Museum gives you a quick but really interesting look at how the culture in the region has changed over time, from ancient stuff to modern art. It also helps people learn more about the culture through classes and activities, making it an important place for keeping the culture alive and helping people understand it better, whether they're from here or just visiting.

How to get there

To reach The Negros Museum, you can follow these general directions:

From Silay Airport, you can ride in a taxi to this location but it's expensive. Take a short walk to the Airport's main gate, then ride in a tricycle going to Silay Highway. At the highway, beside BDO take a van going to Bacolod Terminal, then take a jeep going to the Provincial Capitol. Negros Museum is almost in front of the capitol.
