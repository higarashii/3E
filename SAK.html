Sak-a Falls

Sak-a Falls is a stunning three-tiered waterfall located in San Antonio, Remedios T. Romualdez, Agusan del Norte. It is a natural wonder that offers a refreshing retreat amidst the lush greenery of the Philippine countryside. The first tier of Sak-a Falls is known for its strong flow, which cascades down with vigor, making it a sight to behold but not ideal for swimming due to safety concerns
The second and third tiers of Sak-a Falls, however, are a haven for water enthusiasts. These levels provide a more tranquil experience, with deep, cool waters that are perfect for swimming and cliff jumping. Surrounded by tall, verdant trees, the falls offer a serene environment where visitors can relax and enjoy the beauty of nature. The sparkling water, especially at noon, creates a mesmerizing effect as it reflects the sun’s rays, making Sak-a Falls a picturesque destination for both adventure seekers and those looking to unwind

How to get there

To reach the enchanting Sak-a Falls in Agusan del Norte, start your journey from Butuan City. Here, you can catch a jeepney bound for the town of Remedios T. Romualdez (RTR). Once you arrive in RTR’s town proper, look for a habal-habal—a local motorcycle taxi—to take you to Barangay San Antonio. It’s advisable to hire a local guide in Barangay San Antonio to accompany you on the trek to Sak-a Falls. The guide will lead you through a scenic route that may take around 1 to 2 hours of hiking, depending on your pace. The trail is an adventure in itself, offering a glimpse of the region’s natural beauty before you arrive at the breathtaking falls.
