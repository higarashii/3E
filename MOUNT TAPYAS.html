Mount Tapyas

Mount Tapyas is a prominent mountain and popular tourist attraction in Coron, Palawan, Philippines. Rising 210 meters above sea level, it offers breathtaking panoramic views of Coron town, the surrounding islands, and the sparkling waters of Coron Bay. The highlight of the visit is the iconic giant cross situated at the summit, accessible via a challenging but rewarding climb up a staircase with over 700 steps. The lush greenery and refreshing breeze make Mount Tapyas a picturesque location for both adventure seekers and those seeking a tranquil spot to enjoy the scenic beauty of Palawan.

The history of Mount Tapyas is deeply rooted in Coron's cultural and religious heritage. The mountain has long been considered a sacred site by the local Tagbanua people. The construction of the giant cross, which stands as a symbol of faith and devotion, began in the early 2000s. Mount Tapyas has since become a pilgrimage site and a must-visit destination for tourists, attracting visitors with its stunning views and offering a connection to the cultural and spiritual fabric of Coron.

How to get there

From Coron Town Proper, you can easily flag down a tricycle to take you to the trailhead for only 150 PHP.

Another way to get to Mount Tapyas is on foot and the starting point can be found right next to ‘Davens Palace’ in Coron Town Proper.

‍ From the center of Coron Town, it’s no more than a 10-minute walk to reach the Tapyas stairway.
