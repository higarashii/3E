Mangyan Village

Mangyan Village offers a unique opportunity to experience the indigenous culture of the Mangyan people in Oriental Mindoro. This village provides a glimpse into the traditional way of life, including their customs, crafts, and agricultural practices. Visitors can immerse themselves in the rich cultural heritage of the Mangyan community while exploring the village and interacting with its residents.

Mangyan Village may offer basic amenities such as rest areas, souvenir shops selling handmade crafts, and guided tours conducted by knowledgeable locals. Visitors are encouraged to respect the cultural norms and traditions of the Mangyan people during their visit.

While accommodations are not available within the village itself, visitors can find lodging options in nearby towns such as Calapan City or Puerto Galera. These accommodations range from budget guesthouses to mid-range resorts, offering comfortable stays for travelers. Activities in the area may include guided tours of the village, cultural performances, and workshops on traditional Mangyan crafts.

How to get there

To reach Mangyan Village from Mindoro, visitors can take a local tricycle or taxi from their location to the nearest port. From there, they can catch a ferry or boat to Oriental Mindoro.

Once in Oriental Mindoro, travelers can hire a local guide or rent a vehicle to reach Mangyan Village, which is located in the mountainous regions of the province.
