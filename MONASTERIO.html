Monasterio de Tarlac

The Monasterio de Tarlac is a Catholic monastery on top of Mount Resurrection, part of the Zambales Mountain Range on the island of Luzon in the Philippines.

It is part of the Mount Resurrection Eco Park in Barangay Lubigan, San Jos√©, Tarlac. It houses a relic believed to be a fragment of the True Cross of Jesus.

The Monasterio de Tarlac is a popular pilgrimage site for Catholics and devotees, especially during Holy Week and other religious observances. Many pilgrims climb the 278-step staircase leading to the base of the Risen Christ statue as an act of devotion and penance.

In addition to its religious significance, the Monasterio de Tarlac offers visitors a peaceful and serene environment for prayer, meditation, and reflection. The monastery grounds feature landscaped gardens, prayer areas, and stations of the cross, providing opportunities for spiritual contemplation and renewal.

How to get there

From Manila, take the North Luzon Expressway, exit Sta. Ines Toll Plaza in Mabalacat, Pampanga. Proceed north to Tarlac City. Upon reaching Brgy. San Sebastian (Hon Kee Tea House on your right or Vilmar Restaurant on your left), take a left turn to the bypass road (Tarlac - Pangasinan) towards the town of Camiling. Follow the 36-km backcountry but well-paved road to Brgy. Lubigan in San Jose, Tarlac (turns are well signposted). The monastery is located at the heart of the 278-ha Tarlac Ecotourism Park in Brgy. Lubigan, San Jose, Tarlac. :

By Public Transportation:

1. If you prefer public transportation, you can take a bus bound for San Jose, Tarlac from Tarlac City.

2. Ask the conductor or driver to drop you off at the nearest point to the Monasterio de Tarlac. From there, you may need to take a tricycle or other local transportation to reach the monastery.

By Private Vehicle:

1. If you're driving, you can use GPS or online maps to navigate to the Monasterio de Tarlac.

2. The monastery is located in Barangay Lubigan, San Jose, Tarlac. You can enter this destination into your GPS or mapping app for directions.

Once you arrive in the vicinity, watch for signs or ask locals for directions to the Monasterio de Tarlac.
