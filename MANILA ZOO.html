Manila zoo

The Manila Zoo, officially known as the Manila Zoological and Botanical Garden, is a popular zoological park located in Malate, Manila, Philippines. The Manila Zoo was established on July 25, 1959, and is one of the oldest zoos in Asia.The zoo features various animal exhibits, including mammals, birds, reptiles, and fish. Visitors can see animals such as lions, tigers, elephants, giraffes, crocodiles, and more.

In addition to its zoological collection, the Manila Zoo has a botanical garden that showcases a variety of plants and trees.

How to get there

To reach Manila zoo, you can follow these general directions:

1.LRT-1 (Light Rail Transit Line 1): If you're coming from areas serviced by the LRT-1, such as Baclaran or Monumento, you can ride the train and disembark at the Vito Cruz Station. From there, you can take a jeepney or a short walk to Manila Zoo.

2.Jeepneys are a popular mode of transportation in Manila. Look for jeepneys with routes passing through Malate or Taft Avenue. Inform the driver that you're heading to Manila Zoo, and they can drop you off nearby.

3.Bus: Buses ply various routes within Manila. Look for buses with routes passing through Malate or Taft Avenue. You may need to take another mode of transportation (jeepney or tricycle) for the last part of the journey.
