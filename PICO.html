Pico De loro

Pico de Loro is a popular mountain located in the province of Cavite in the Philippines. Its name, which translates to "Parrot's Beak" in Spanish, comes from the shape of the peak, which resembles a parrot's beak when viewed from a distance.

This mountain is part of the Mount Palay-Palay National Park and is a favorite destination for hikers and outdoor enthusiasts. The summit offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding landscape, including nearby mountains and the beautiful coastline of Batangas province.

Aside from hiking, Pico de Loro is also known for rock climbing, bird watching, and camping. Its proximity to Metro Manila makes it a convenient destination for day trips or weekend getaways for those looking to escape the city and reconnect with nature.

How to get there

To get to Pico de Loro, you typically start your journey from Manila, the capital city of the Philippines. Here's a general guide on how to get there:

1.Public Transportation: From Manila, you can take a bus bound for Ternate, Cavite. Several bus companies operate routes to Ternate from various terminals in Manila. Make sure to inform the bus conductor or driver that you're heading to Pico de Loro so they can drop you off at the correct stop.

2.Private Transportation: If you prefer more flexibility and convenience, you can drive to Pico de Loro using your own vehicle or by renting one. From Manila, take the Coastal Road or the Manila-Cavite Expressway (CAVITEX) and head towards Ternate, Cavite. From Ternate, follow the signs leading to the Mount Palay-Palay National Park where Pico de Loro is located.

3.Tricycle or Jeepney Ride: Once you reach Ternate, you'll need to take a tricycle or jeepney ride to the jump-off point for Pico de Loro. The jump-off point is typically at the Magnetic Hill or DENR Station. Tricycle drivers in Ternate are familiar with the location and can take you there.

4.Hike to Pico de Loro: From the jump-off point, you'll begin your hike to Pico de Loro. The hike usually takes around 2-4 hours depending on your pace and the trail conditions. It's advisable to start early in the morning to avoid the midday heat and to allow ample time to enjoy the summit views.
