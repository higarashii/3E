Blue Water Cave

Blue Water Cave in Baggao, Cagayan, entices adventurers with its mysterious allure and striking azure waters, making it a must-visit destination.

Accessible through a moderate trek amidst verdant foliage, the cave unveils its enchanting beauty as visitors navigate its winding passages.

Inside, the tranquil underground river mesmerizes with its crystal-clear waters, providing a refreshing escape from the outside world.

Surrounding rock formations adorned with stalactites and stalagmites add to the cave's surreal atmosphere, evoking a sense of awe and wonder.

Blue Water Cave promises an unforgettable experience, inviting explorers to immerse themselves in the natural splendor of Cagayan's hidden treasures.

How to get there

To reach St. Peter's Metropolitan Cathedral, you can follow these general directions:

By Public Transportation:

From Manila

1. Travel to Tuguegarao City by bus from major bus terminals such as Cubao or Pasay in Manila. Several bus companies operate routes to Tuguegarao.

2. Once in Tuguegarao City, transfer to a bus bound for Baggao using the same process outlined above.

3. Upon arriving in Baggao town proper, you'll need to find transportation to the trailhead leading to Blue Water Cave. This may involve hiring a tricycle or motorbike to take you to the starting point of the trek.

By Private Vehicle:

By Car or Motorcycle

1. If you have your own vehicle, you can drive to Baggao from Tuguegarao City or other nearby towns. Follow the Maharlika Highway (AH26) to reach Baggao.

2. Once in Baggao, navigate to the trailhead leading to Blue Water Cave. This may require asking locals for directions or using GPS/navigation apps to locate the starting point of the trek.

3. Regardless of the mode of transportation you choose, it's advisable to plan your trip in advance, especially if you're traveling long distances. Additionally, consider factors such as traffic conditions and weather when planning your journey.
