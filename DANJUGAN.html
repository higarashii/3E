Danjugan Island

Danjugan Island is a pristine gem located in Brgy. Bulata, Cauayan, Negros Occidental, Philippines.

It acts as a sanctuary for several species of fish, birds, and reptiles because of its colorful coral reefs, lush mangrove forests, and diverse marine life. For those who love the outdoors and the environment and are looking for peace and adventure among untouched beauty, the island is a haven.

Danjugan Island's abundant biodiversity can be discovered by guests on guided nature walks, snorkeling trips, and diving explorations. While endangered bird species like the Philippine cockatoo find refuge in the dense jungle, visitors can catch glimpses of vibrant coral gardens brimming with tropical fish in the crystal-clear waters around the island. Danjugan Island, which is a conservation area, is not only a beautiful place to visit but also evidence of how important it is to protect our natural heritage for the enjoyment of future generations.

How to get there

To reach Danjugan Island, you can follow these general directions:

The nearest airport to Danjugan Island is Bacolod-Silay Airport, which is located in Bacolod City. From there, you can take a bus or a car to the town of Bulata, which is the jump-off point for trips to the island. From Bulata, you will need to take a boat to reach the island. The trip takes about an hour and a half by pumpboat.
