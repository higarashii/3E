Gabaldon Falls

It is not an exaggeration to call Gabaldon Falls a “hidden paradise” at the bosom of Mt. Mingan – a breathtaking water fall that dominates the upper portion of the river, with unusually large and attractive boulders that adorn its banks and the river bed, plus lush vegetation of wild flowers, ferns, different varieties of trees along its banks and serves as habitat for diverse wild animals especially birds, lizards, butterflies and the like. The place is a must see when you visit Gabaldon. When you get there, you will enjoy its clear cold and clear water. Many of the tourists enjoy jumping from the upstream of the falls to the plunge pool or dabble or wade in the stream. It is a one-of-a-kind experience to enjoy the beauty of nature far from the city life. Visiting the place could release and sooth tension and stress away. Tourists find the place ideal for contemplating and communicating with nature.

Nueva Ecija has always been known as the Rice Granary of the Philippines. Famous for its vast plains of rice fields, people often overlook what else the land has to offer. Unbeknownst toFilipinos aside from those who live in the region, the provinceboasts of grand mountains and rivers, and exotic flora and fauna, along with a rich culture of Catholic, and military history.To top it all off, Nueva Ecija also has an excellent culinary scene that pushes the norms with the likes of carabao milk,and tilapia ice cream.

How to get there

To reach Gabaldon Falls , you can follow these general directions:

By Public Transportation:

1. You can take a bus bound for Gabaldon, Nueva Ecija. Buses to Cabanatuan City or Dingalan pass through Gabaldon. Once in Gabaldon, you'll need to hire a tricycle or a habal-habal (motorcycle taxi) to take you to the falls.

By Private Vehicle:

1. If you're driving, you can take the Nueva Ecija-Aurora Road. Gabaldon Falls is located near the town proper of Gabaldon, and there are signs along the road directing you to the falls. Parking may be available near the entrance.

You can take a bus from Manila to Cabanatuan City, then transfer to a Gabaldon-bound jeepney or van. Once in Gabaldon, hire local transportation to take you to the falls.
