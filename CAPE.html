Cape Engaño Lighthouse

Cape Engaño Lighthouse, standing proudly on the rugged cliffs of Palaui Island in Cagayan, Philippines, is not just a beacon guiding ships through treacherous waters; it's a silent witness to centuries of history and maritime adventures. Built by the Spanish colonial government in 1892, it serves as a testament to their dedication to safe navigation in the archipelago. What makes this lighthouse truly unique is its architectural style, blending Spanish and American influences, reflecting the complex history of the Philippines.

The lighthouse offers stunning panoramic views of the Luzon Strait and the surrounding lush landscapes, making it a favorite spot for photographers and adventurers alike. It has survived numerous natural calamities and even World War II, standing resilient against the passage of time.

The isolation of Palaui Island adds to the allure of the lighthouse, as reaching it involves a boat ride and a trek through unspoiled wilderness, offering visitors a sense of discovery and adventure. Today, Cape Engaño Lighthouse stands not only as a symbol of maritime safety but also as a cultural and historical landmark, drawing visitors from around the world to experience its timeless charm.

How to get there

To reach Cape Engaño Lighthouse, you can follow these general directions:

1. Start by getting to Santa Ana, the nearest town to Palaui Island. You can reach Santa Ana by land from Tuguegarao City, the capital of Cagayan province. Tuguegarao is accessible by bus or plane from Manila.

2. Once in Santa Ana, you'll need to arrange transportation to Palaui Island. You can hire a boat from San Vicente Port in Santa Ana, which is the main jump-off point to Palaui Island.

3. Board a boat from San Vicente Port to Palaui Island. The boat ride typically takes around 30 to 45 minutes, depending on sea conditions.

4. Upon reaching Palaui Island, you'll arrive at Punta Verde, the main entry point to the island. This is where you'll register and pay the necessary fees for visiting the island.

5. From Punta Verde, you'll embark on a scenic trek through Palaui's lush forests and rugged terrain. The trek to Cape Engaño Lighthouse usually takes around 1 to 1.5 hours, depending on your pace and the weather conditions.

6. Once you reach Cape Engaño Lighthouse, take some time to admire its beauty and soak in the breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and the sea.

7. After exploring the lighthouse and its surroundings, retrace your steps back to Punta Verde. From there, board your boat back to San Vicente Port in Santa Ana.

8. Once back in Santa Ana, you can either stay overnight in the town or travel back to your accommodation elsewhere, depending on your travel plans.

Remember to check weather conditions and sea advisories before embarking on your journey to Palaui Island, as rough seas can affect boat travel. It's also advisable to bring essentials such as water, snacks, sunscreen, and insect repellent for the trek.
