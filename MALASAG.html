The Malasag Eco-tourism Village

Nestled amidst the lush greenery of Misamis Oriental in the Philippines, the Malasag Eco-Tourism Village stands as a beacon of sustainable tourism and cultural preservation. Tucked away in the foothills of Cagayan de Oro City, this enchanting sanctuary offers visitors a serene escape into the heart of nature while celebrating the rich heritage of the indigenous tribes of Mindanao.

As you step into the village, you are greeted by the harmonious blend of modern eco-friendly amenities and traditional Filipino architecture. Thatched-roof cottages and bamboo huts dot the landscape, providing cozy accommodations that seamlessly integrate with the surrounding environment. Each abode offers a glimpse into the local way of life, with indigenous-inspired decor and furnishings crafted by skilled artisans.

At the heart of Malasag Eco-Tourism Village lies a deep respect for the indigenous cultures that call this land home. Visitors are invited to partake in cultural presentations and demonstrations, where traditional dances, music, and crafts are showcased with pride. Through engaging workshops and interactions with local communities, guests gain a deeper appreciation for the rich tapestry of Mindanao's heritage.

In essence, Malasag Eco-Tourism Village beckons travelers to embark on a journey of discovery, where the wonders of nature and the vibrant culture of the Philippines converge in perfect harmony. Whether seeking adventure, relaxation, or a deeper connection with the land and its people, this hidden gem offers an unforgettable experience that nourishes the soul and leaves a lasting impact on the heart.

How to get there

To reach The Malasag Eco-tourism Village, you can follow these general directions:

By Public Transportation:

1. Head to the Agora Market Terminal or the Eastbound Terminal.

2. Look for a jeepney or bus bound for Barangay Cugman or Indahag. Confirm with the driver or conductor that they pass by or near Malasag Eco-Tourism Village.

3. Alight at the nearest point to the village entrance. From there, you may need to take a tricycle to reach the village itself.

By Private Vehicle:

1. If you're traveling by private vehicle from nearby towns or cities, you can use online maps or navigation apps to guide you to The Malasag Eco-tourism Village.

2. Head east from the city center, following the direction towards Barangay Cugman.

3. You can take Masterson Avenue or the National Highway, depending on your starting point.

4. Once you reach Barangay Cugman, continue towards Malasag Road. There should be signs indicating the direction to Malasag Eco-Tourism Village.

5. Follow Malasag Road until you reach the entrance of the village.

It's recommended to verify the specific routes and transportation options based on your starting point and current travel conditions. Additionally, always exercise caution and follow local traffic regulations while traveling.
