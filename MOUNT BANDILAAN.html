Mount Bandilaan National Park

Mount Bandilaan National Park Located in Mount Bandilaan, Siquijor, Philippines. It is in the midst of Siquijor Island, Mt Bandilaan is a stunning 212 hectares of Forest Reserve declared in 1940 under Presidential Proclamation 523 by then President Manuel L. Quezon. It rises 557 meters above sea level and is the highest peak in the province. On its apex stands a 15 meter tall viewing tower where nature lovers can enjoy a 360 degree panoramic view of the entire island while bathing in the breeze of fresh cool air.

It is the biggest watershed of the island and home to numerous flora and fauna including the endangered Hypsipetes Siquijorensis a streak-breasted Bulbul endemic in Siquijor. Mt. Bandilaan is the source of various medicinal trees and plants used by the traditional or folk healers of the island and the venue of the famous “Pahi-uli”: Siquijor Healing Festival celebrated during the Holy Week.

How to get there

For you to be able to reach the tourist destination, if you're traveling from outside of Siquijor, you can fly to Dumaguete City Airport (DGT) in Negros Oriental. From there, you can take a taxi or tricycle to Dumaguete Port and catch a ferry to Siquijor. Once you arrive in Siquijor, you can hire a tricycle or motorcycle taxi to take you to Mount Bandilaan National Park. You can also take a ferry directly from Cebu City to Siquijor. Several ferry companies operate this route, and the journey takes around 4-6 hours, depending on the ferry schedule and sea conditions. Once in Siquijor, you can hire a tricycle or motorcycle taxi to reach the national park. If you prefer more independence in exploring the island, you can rent a car or motorcycle in Siquijor. Several rental agencies offer this service, and you can arrange for pick-up/drop-off at the port or your accommodation. With your rented vehicle, you can drive to Mount Bandilaan National Park at your own pace.

Once you arrive at Mount Bandilaan National Park, you can explore its lush forests, hiking trails, and various attractions, including the butterfly sanctuary, grotto, and viewpoint.
