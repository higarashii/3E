Mount Data National Park

Mount Data National Park is a picturesque natural haven. The exact address of Mount Data National Park is in Bauko, Mountain Province, Luzon Cordilleras, Philippines. The park spans approximately 5,513 hectares (13,620 acres) and is located along the Halsema Highway, about 100 kilometers (62 miles) north of Baguio City.

This national park is renowned for its breathtaking landscapes, encompassing verdant pine forests, mossy oak forests, and rolling hills. The park is also home to five major rivers, including the Chico, Ahin, Siffu, Abra, and Amburayan rivers, which add to its natural beauty.

Mount Data National Park offers tourists a stunning natural environment with lush mossy and pine forests, making it a haven for nature lovers and hikers. Visitors can explore the park's diverse flora and fauna, enjoy scenic views from its elevated location, and experience the tranquility of this protected area in the Philippines.

How to get there

To reach Mount Data National Park, you can follow these general directions:

By Public Transportation:

1. Take a bus from Manila to Baguio City. The bus journey takes around 6-7 hours, and the fare ranges from PHP 450 to PHP 750.

2. From Baguio, take a bus or jeepney bound for Bontoc. The travel time is approximately 6-7 hours.Ask the driver to drop you off at Mount Data National Park or the nearest point of access. Ensure you confirm the drop-off point with the driver before your journey.

By Private Vehicle:

1. Drive from Manila to Baguio City, then continue towards Bontoc. Use navigation apps like Waze or Google Maps to guide you to Mount Data National Park.

2. Upon reaching the park, there are designated parking areas where you can park your private vehicle before exploring the park.

Whether you option for public transportation or a private vehicle, following these directions will help you reach Mount Data National Park conveniently in the CAR region.
