Bomod-ok Falls

Bomod-ok Falls is a stunning natural attraction located in the Mountain Province of the Philippines, specifically in Sagada.The falls are situated along the A Guid-Pide-Fideli San Trail in Sagada, 2619 Mountain Province, Philippines. It is often referred to as the "Mother Falls" of Sagada due to its significance in providing life-giving water to the rice terraces of Pide and Fidelisan.

The falls themselves are fed by the Am Lusong tributary, with mountain waters diving into a plunge pool filled with rocks and boulders. The depth of the plunge pool varies depending on the season, adding to the dynamic beauty of Bomod-ok Falls. Despite the treacherous hike and winding trails, tourists from all over the country continue to visit this waterfall to witness its magnificence.

Bomod-ok Falls in Mountain Province offers tourists a breathtaking natural attraction surrounded by picturesque rice terraces and mountains. Visitors can enjoy a refreshing dip in the ice-cold water, take stunning photographs, and experience the beauty of nature in this serene and tranquil setting.

How to get there

To reach Bomod-ok Falls, you can follow these general directions:

By Public Transportation:

1. Take a bus from Manila to Sagada. Several bus companies offer trips to Sagada from locations like Cubao or Pasay. The journey takes around 12 hours.

2. Once in Sagada, head to the tourism information center in the town proper. Register and pay the necessary tourism fees. Arrange for a guided tour to Bomod-ok Falls at the tourism office. A local guide will be assigned to you for the hike to the falls.

By Private Vehicle:

1. Drive from Manila to Sagada via Baguio. The journey takes approximately 12-13 hours, depending on traffic and road conditions. Utilize navigation apps like Waze or Google Maps for the best route.

2. Once in Sagada, there are designated parking areas where you can park your private vehicle before starting the hike to Bomod-ok Falls.

Whether you choose public transportation or a private vehicle, these guidelines will help you navigate your way to Bomod-ok Falls in the CAR region effectively.
