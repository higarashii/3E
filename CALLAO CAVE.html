Callao Cave

Callao Cave is one of the most famous natural landmarks in the Cagayan Valley region of the Philippines. Located in Barangays Magdalo and Quibal in the municipality of Peñablanca, Callao Cave is renowned for its unique rock formations, cathedral-like chambers, and its historical and cultural significance.

The cave complex consists of seven chambers, with the first chamber serving as the main entrance. What makes Callao Cave truly special is its natural skylight created by a large opening in the cave ceiling, allowing sunlight to filter through and illuminate parts of the cavern, creating a surreal and picturesque sight.

Aside from its natural beauty, Callao Cave holds cultural significance as well. Archaeological excavations have revealed evidence of early human habitation dating back thousands of years. Inside the cave, ancient artifacts such as pottery shards and tools have been discovered, shedding light on the prehistoric inhabitants of the area.

Moreover, Callao Cave is a popular destination for religious pilgrimages and spiritual retreats. Within the caverns, visitors can find a chapel dedicated to the Virgin Mary, complete with a cross and religious icons. This adds another layer of significance to the cave, making it not only a natural wonder but also a place of reverence and worship for many.

Visitors to Callao Cave can explore its chambers through guided tours offered by local guides. The cave's awe-inspiring beauty, combined with its rich history and cultural importance, makes it a must-visit destination for travelers exploring the Cagayan Valley region.

How to get there

To reach Callao Cave, you can follow these general directions:

By Public Transportation:

1. From Manila to Tuguegarao City: Take a bus bound for Tuguegarao City, Cagayan. Several bus companies operate routes from various terminals in Manila to Tuguegarao. The journey typically takes around 10 to 12 hours, depending on traffic and road conditions.

2. From Tuguegarao City to Peñablanca: Upon reaching Tuguegarao City, head to the van terminal near the city center. Vans bound for Peñablanca, where Callao Cave is located, are available. The travel time is approximately 30 to 45 minutes.

3. From Peñablanca to Callao Cave: From the Peñablanca town proper, you can hire a tricycle or motorcycle (locally known as habal-habal) to take you to Callao Cave. It's a short ride from the town center to the cave entrance.

By Private Vehicle:

1. From Manila to Tuguegarao City: Take the North Luzon Expressway (NLEX) and continue onto the Subic-Clark-Tarlac Expressway (SCTEX). From there, merge onto the Tarlac-Pangasinan-La Union Expressway (TPLEX) and follow signs leading to the North Luzon provinces until you reach Tuguegarao City.

2. From Tuguegarao City to Peñablanca: From Tuguegarao City, take the Maharlika Highway (also known as Pan-Philippine Highway) heading north towards the municipality of Peñablanca. The distance is approximately 20 kilometers.

3. From Peñablanca to Callao Cave: Once you reach Peñablanca, follow the main road towards the town center. Look for signs indicating the way to Callao Cave. The cave entrance is easily accessible by car, and there are parking facilities available.

Regardless of the mode of transportation you choose, it's always a good idea to check for any updates on routes, schedules, and road conditions before embarking on your journey. Additionally, it's advisable to visit Callao Cave during daylight hours to fully appreciate its beauty and ensure a safe and enjoyable experience.
