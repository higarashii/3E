Sulu Provincial Capitol

The capitol is located near the Sulu State College and is Islam inspired, this is because of the fact that 97% of the population are Tausugs who embrace Islam as their religion.

Located at the heart of Jolo, the capital town of the province, the Sulu Provincial Capitol with its Moorish inspired architectural design and golden domes is considered as one of the most beautiful capitol buildings in the country.

The two-story building is a timeless legacy of the late Sulu Gov Murphy Sangkula. Further, it is composed of the main art deco concrete façade then topped off by an octagonal structure that ends in a gold-painted hemispherical dome.

Exploring the capitol building of Sulu almost brought me to another world. I felt like I was out of the Philippines. But I had to accept that beautiful magnificent structures like this really exist in our country.

How to get there

To reach Sulu Provincial Capitol, you can follow these general directions:

By Public Transportation:

1.Travel to Jolo: First, you’ll need to reach the island of Jolo. You can take a flight to Jolo Airport or travel by boat from nearby islands.

2.From Jolo Airport: If you arrive by air, you can hire a tricycle or a local taxi to take you directly to the Sulu Provincial Capitol. It’s approximately 3 kilometers from the airport.

3.From the Port: If you arrive by boat, head to the Jolo port. From there, you can take a tricycle or a jeepney to reach the provincial capitol.
