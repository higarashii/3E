Echo Valley

Nestled within the picturesque landscapes of Mountain Province in the Cordillera Administrative Region (CAR), Echo Valley offers visitors a serene escape into nature. Located near the town of Sagada, Echo Valley is renowned for its tranquil atmosphere, lush greenery, and captivating limestone cliffs. The valley derives its name from the natural echo produced by its towering rock formations, adding to its mystical allure. Echo Valley is not only a haven for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts but also holds cultural significance as it is home to the renowned Sagada Hanging Coffins, an ancient burial tradition of the local Igorot people.

Visitors to Echo Valley can immerse themselves in the breathtaking scenery, explore hiking trails amidst verdant forests, and discover the rich cultural heritage of the region. Echo Valley is situated near the town of Sagada in Mountain Province, CAR, Philippines. Travelers can reach Sagada by bus or private vehicle from major cities such as Baguio or Manila. Once in Sagada town proper, Echo Valley is accessible by foot or guided tour. The valley is located within walking distance from the town center, and visitors can embark on a scenic hike through picturesque trails to reach the site.

Alternatively, guided tours are available for those who prefer a more structured exploration of Echo Valley and its surrounding attractions. While Echo Valley itself does not offer accommodation options, visitors can find a variety of lodging choices in nearby Sagada town. From cozy guesthouses and homestays to comfortable hotels and lodges, Sagada provides a range of accommodation options to suit every traveler's preferences and budget.

Additionally, travelers can enjoy a multitude of activities in and around Echo Valley, including guided tours to the Sagada Hanging Coffins, nature walks through the valley's lush forests, birdwatching, and photography. Adventurous souls can also explore nearby caves such as Sumaguing Cave, renowned for its stunning rock formations and underground rivers. With its natural beauty and cultural significance, Echo Valley offers visitors a truly immersive experience in the heart of Mountain Province.

How to get there

By Air:

The nearest airport is in Baguio City, the Loakan Airport. From Baguio, take a bus or hire a private vehicle to Sagada. Echo Valley is a short trek from Sagada town proper.

By Land:

Visitors may hire a private vehicle to Sagada. Echo Valley is one of the attractions within Sagada, accessible by foot from the town center.
