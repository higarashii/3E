Mount Pinatubo Crater Lake

Located at Barangay Santa Juliana, Capas, Tarlac, Philippines.

Mount Pinatubo Crater Lake is situated within the crater of Mount Pinatubo, a dormant volcano located on the island of Luzon. The volcano is part of the Zambales Mountain Range and is nestled between the provinces of Zambales, Tarlac, and Pampanga.

Mount Pinatubo Crater Lake is renowned for its breathtaking beauty, with its turquoise-blue waters contrasting against the surrounding rugged terrain. The lake's serene ambiance and dramatic landscape make it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, nature lovers, and adventure seekers.

Once at the crater lake, visitors can relax and enjoy the serene surroundings, swim in the cool waters, or rent a small boat for a leisurely ride around the lake. Swimming in the crater lake is allowed, but visitors should exercise caution and adhere to safety guidelines, as the lake's depth and underwater hazards can vary.

How to get there

The journey to Mount Pinatubo Crater Lake usually begins in Manila, the capital city of the Philippines.

By Public Transportation:

1. If you prefer public transportation, you can take a bus bound for Tarlac City or Capas, Tarlac from major bus terminals in Manila, such as Victory Liner or Genesis Transport.

2. From Tarlac City or Capas, you can then take a tricycle or jeepney to Barangay Santa Juliana, the jump-off point for the trek to Mount Pinatubo.

By Private Vehicle:

1. The journey to Mount Pinatubo Crater Lake usually begins in Manila, the capital city of the Philippines. You can drive or hire a private vehicle to take you to the base camp in Capas, Tarlac.

2. The drive usually takes around 3 to 4 hours, depending on traffic conditions.

Along the way, you'll traverse volcanic terrain, rocky paths, and streams, enjoying breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
