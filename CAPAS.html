Capas National Shrine

Capas National Shrine is located at Capas Shrine Museum, New Clark City, Capas, Tarlac, Philippines.

The Capas National Shrine is a 70-meter-high, black obelisk hovering over the 54 hectares of parkland planted with rows of trees to represent each of the dead.

This monument was built in the province of Tarlac to honor the brave men and women who defied the might of the invaders at Bataan during World War II. Along with this, its location is where the infamous 97-kilometer march or the Bataan Death March ended.

The center of attraction in the whole shrine is the black obelisk, but it is not your basic four-cornered obelisks; it is segmented into three posts that meet at the top. Surrounding the obelisk are three walls or the “Wall of Heroes Memorial,” engraved on these walls are some of the names of identified Filipino soldiers who died during the march.

How to get there

From Manila, take the North Luzon Expressway (NLEX) and exit at the Santa Ines Toll Plaza in Mabalacat, Pampanga. Continue along MacArthur Highway until you reach Capas, Tarlac. From Capas town proper, follow the signs leading to the shrine, which is located in Barangay O'Donnell. :

By Public Transportation:

1. you can take a bus from Manila to Capas, Tarlac.

2. Several bus companies operate routes to Tarlac, with terminals located in various parts of Metro Manila such as Cubao, Pasay, and Sampaloc. From Capas town proper, you can take a tricycle or jeepney to the Capas National Shrine.

By Private Vehicle:

1. If you're driving from Manila, take the North Luzon Expressway (NLEX) and continue northbound towards Tarlac City.

2. From Tarlac City, follow the signs towards Capas. The shrine is located in Barangay Aranguren, Capas, Tarlac. Use a GPS or map application to navigate to the shrine. The approximate travel time by car is around 2 to 3 hours, depending on traffic conditions.

By following these directions, you should be able to reach the Capas National Shrine and pay your respects to the fallen soldiers who perished during the Bataan Death March.
