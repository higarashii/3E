Capisaan Cave System in Nueva Vizcaya

Nestled within the rugged landscapes of Nueva Vizcaya, the Capisaan Cave System stands as a remarkable testament to the province’s geological and natural wonders. Encompassing a vast network of caves, tunnels, and chambers, this intricate labyrinth beckons adventurers and explorers from around the world to uncover its mysteries. Carved over millennia by the forces of nature, the Capisaan Cave System offers a fascinating glimpse into the Earth’s history, with its stunning limestone formations, underground rivers, and hidden passages captivating the imagination of all who venture within. As one of the most extensive cave systems in the Philippines, the Capisaan Cave System presents a thrilling challenge for spelunkers and outdoor enthusiasts alike. Exploring its depths promises an unforgettable adventure, with each cavern revealing unique geological formations and ecological wonders. From towering stalactites and stalagmites to subterranean pools and chambers adorned with rare rock formations, every corner of the Capisaan Cave System holds the potential for discovery and awe-inspiring encounters with nature’s grandeur. Beyond its natural beauty, the Capisaan Cave System also holds cultural and historical significance for the indigenous communities of Nueva Vizcaya. These caves have served as sanctuaries, meeting places, and even burial sites for generations, bearing witness to the rich heritage and traditions of the region. Today, efforts are underway to preserve and protect this invaluable natural treasure, ensuring that future generations can continue to marvel at the wonders of the Capisaan Cave System for years to come.

How to get there

To reach Capisaan Cave System in Nueva Vizcaya, you can follow these general directions:

Exploring the Capisaan Cave System is an adventure of a lifetime, but reaching this hidden gem requires some planning and navigation. From Manila, travelers can take a bus bound for Bayombong, the capital of Nueva Vizcaya, which is approximately a 6 to 7-hour journey. Upon reaching Bayombong, visitors can then hire a private vehicle or take a public van or jeepney to the town of Kasibu, where the Capisaan Cave System is located. From Kasibu, it’s advisable to arrange for a local guide who can lead the way to the caves and ensure a safe and memorable exploration experience.
