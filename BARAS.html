Baras Cave

One interesting place that visitors usually skip is a small cave named Baras in Brgy. Lawi in the Municipality of Jordan. Baras Cave is a small cave with two openings. One can practically swim from one entrance to the other. The water is quite deep, while its reflection on the interior of the cave creates a mesmerizing effect. Small cave with double entry for a short break visiting the charming islands of Guimaras. Nice for swimming, break from the sun and talking some photos.

Baras Cave is one of the stops at the Islang Hopping in Guimaras arranged by the Raymen Beach Resort. It is a spacious cave with different rock formations already. It is possible and safe to go deeper inside but better with a guide. Guimaras has a number of caves that tourists can explore, all of them varying in accessibility and size.

The most visited is the Baras Cave, which houses fruit bats. Other caves, such as Biri Cave and Bucoy Cave - both in Jordan - can only be accessed by crawling. Capitoguan Cave, also in Jordan, is a bat sanctuary and is about 250 meters (820 feet) long and the entrance is 12 meters (39 feet) tall. Higante Cave, found on top of a hill in Jordan, was said to have been inhabited by a giant, hence its name.

How to get there

To reach Baras Cave, you can follow these general directions:

From Manila, take a flight to IloIlo. Travel time is roughly 1hr. and 25 minutes. From IloIlo International Airport, take a point to point bus to IloIlo City proper. From downtown IloIlo City, you may take a jeepney or taxi to Ortiz Wharf.

Get in line and purchase your ferry/boat ticket to Jordan Wharf. Board your boat to Jordan Point in Guimaras. Travel time is about 15 mins. Take a jeepney or tricycle to your resort. Ride a boat to go to the Baras Cave.
