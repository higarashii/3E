Mount Kalawitan

Mount Kalawitan is a majestic mountain located in the province of Mountain Province, Philippines. It is part of the Cordillera Central mountain range, specifically situated in the municipality of Barlig.With an elevation of approximately 2,714 meters (8,901 feet) above sea level, Mount Kalawitan is one of the prominent peaks in the region. It offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes, including lush forests, rolling hills, and neighboring mountains.

The mountain is known for its challenging yet rewarding hiking trails, attracting outdoor enthusiasts and adventure seekers. The ascent to the summit requires a moderate to difficult level of hiking experience, as it involves steep and rugged terrains. However, the journey is well worth it for those who are up to the challenge, as it offers an opportunity to immerse in the beauty of nature and experience a sense of accomplishment.Along the trail, hikers can witness the diverse flora and fauna of the area, including mossy forests, endemic plant species, and various bird species. The mountain is also home to indigenous communities, and hikers may have the chance to interact with the locals and learn about their culture and traditions.
