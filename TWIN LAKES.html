Balinsasayao Twin Lakes Natural Park

The Balinsasayao Twin Lakes Natural Park is located in the province of Negros Oriental, Philippines. Specifically, it is situated in the towns of Sibulan, Valencia, and San Jose. The park is approximately 14.5 kilometers west of Dumaguete, the capital city of Negros Oriental. It encompasses the picturesque Lake Balinsasayao and Lake Danao, which are twin crater lakes situated approximately 14.5 kilometers (9.0 miles) west of Dumaguete. The park spans an expansive area of 8,016.05 hectares (19,808.1 acres) and boasts elevations ranging from 830 to 846 meters (2,723 to 2,776 feet).

This natural park offers a range of amenities and activities for visitors to enjoy. You can embark on boat rides to explore the serene lakes, engage in kayaking adventures, go trekking through the lush surroundings, or even set up camp for a memorable outdoor experience. Boat rides are available at a cost of Php 250 per hour and can accommodate 1-7 persons. Balinsasayao Twin Lakes Natural Park is also home to a diverse array of wildlife and unique flora and fauna, providing nature enthusiasts with a captivating and immersive environment to explore.

How to get there

To reach Balinsasayao Twin Lakes Natural Park, you can follow these general directions:

To reach Balinsasayao Twin Lakes Natural Park in Negros Oriental, there are a few options. One way is to join a full-day tour that includes transportation from Dumaguete City to the park. Another option is to rent a private vehicle and drive to the lakes, which takes about 45 minutes from the city. Alternatively, you can hire a habal-habal or motorcycle taxi to take you from the main highway to the lakes. Once at the park, you can explore the beautiful lakes by paddleboat or kayak, go hiking on the nature trails, and immerse yourself in the serene natural surroundings.
